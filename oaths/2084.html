Oath ID 2084: Xenophon, Anabasis, 2.3.2-10, (literary, Hist., ca.370-355)

  Remarks: The exact oath statement is unknown. It is likely, though not completely certain, that the Persians also swore that they would lead the Greeks to a place where they could get provisions; the wily and cautious Clearchus would be likely to insist on that.
According to Xenophon, the Persian King was terrified of the approach of the Greeks and sent heralds to negotiate a truce.
Clearchus responded (because the Greeks had no provisions), "Report to him that we must have a battle first; for we have not had breakfast, and no one should dare speak to the Greeks about a truce unless he provides them with a breakfast".
The heralds left, and returned quickly with a report that the King thought what they said seemed reasonable, and that they had brought guides with them who could lead the Greeks to a place where they could get provisions if a truce should be concluded.
Clearchus asked whether he was making a truce merely with the men who were coming and going, or whether the truce would bind others as well.
The heralds replied, "every one of them until your message is carried to the King".
Clearchus then took counsel, and it was thought best to conclude a truce quickly so that they could get provisions without being molested.
Clearchus, however, delayed telling the heralds in order to make them afraid, but then concluded the truce after it seemed the proper time had come.
After the truce was concluded the heralds led the Greeks to where they could get provisions.
Xenophon observes that Clearchus, although he had made the truce, kept his army in line of battle on the march, and commanded the rearguard himself.
