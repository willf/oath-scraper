Oath ID 546: Homer, Odyssey, 14.393-400, (literary, Epic., date uncertain)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Ithaca   Location: Eumaeus' shack
 
  Swearer:
Cretan (male, old, free, Cretan)
  Swearee:
Eumaeus (male, mature, chattel-slave, n/a)
 
  Proposed by:
Cretan (male, old, free, unknown)
 
  If taken: The terms will of the agreement will be valid
  If refused: The terms will not be valid
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: see remarks   True: see remarks
  Impact: None: Eumaeus rejects the oath
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That if Odysseus does not return, Eumaeus can kill him, but if Odysseus does return, he will receive a tunic and a mantle, and be sent on his way to Doulichion]
ἀλλ’ ἄγε νῦν ῥήτρην ποιησόμεθ’· αὐτὰρ ὄπισθεν
μάρτυροι ἀμφοτέροισι θεοί, τοὶ Ὄλυμπον ἔχουσιν.
εἰ μέν κεν νοστήσῃ ἄναξ τεὸς ἐς τόδε δῶμα,
ἕσσας με χλαῖνάν τε χιτῶνά τε εἵματα πέμψαι
Δουλίχιόνδ’ ἰέναι, ὅθι μοι φίλον ἔπλετο θυμῷ·
εἰ δέ κε μὴ ἔλθῃσιν ἄναξ τεὸς ὡς ἀγορεύω,
δμῶας ἐπισσεύας βαλέειν μεγάλης κατὰ πέτρης,
ὄφρα καὶ ἄλλος πτωχὸς ἀλεύεται ἠπεροπεύειν.”
 
  Linguistic: martu
 
  God(s) invoked:
The gods    
 
  Remarks: The Cretan proposes an agreement between himself and Eumaeus as follows: if Odysseus returns, the Cretan will be rewarded; but if Odysseus does not return, the Cretan will be killed. The Cretan calls the gods to witness his statement but the agreement is not made since Eumaeus rejects it. Eumaeus is forced to reject the oath because if, as he expects, Odysseus does not return, he will have to choose between a violation of the oath and a violation of the laws of hospitality, with divine punishment a certainty either way. From Odysseus' point of view, of course, that makes the oath a very handy device for testing Eumaeus. This oath is made with reference to the previous oath sworn by the Cretan at oath id 542 (also referred to in the immediately preceding lines at oath id 545), in which he swore that Odysseus would come home.
