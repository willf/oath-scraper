  Remarks: A.H. Sommerstein comments in a personal communication: "Trygaeus, apparently persuaded by the oath of their sincerity, grants them this one high kick – and then they say "But I can't avoid doing the same with the left as well, you know!" (334). That, by itself, might be excused on the plea of force majeure; an oath, like a law, cannot compel one to do the impossible. It will, however, be another matter if they then continue to dance on as if nothing had happened; and certainly their next few words might suggest that they do: "I’m glad, I’m happy, I fart, I laugh, at having escaped from my shield, more than if I’d cast off my old age! (335-6)." However, Trygaeus, though still annoyed with them, no longer complains that they are still dancing, and I think on the whole we are not here entitled to assume that they have violated their oath". On the fluctuating identity of the Chorus in this play, see Sommerstein (ed) xviii-xix.
