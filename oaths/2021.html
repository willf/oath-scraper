Oath ID 2021: Xenophon, Memorabilia 1a, 1.1.18, (literary, Dialogue, 394-385)


  Date: 406
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Bouleuterion
 
  Swearer:
Socrates (male, old, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Presiding magistrate (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: They can take office in the boule
  If refused: They cannot take office in the boule
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: fair judgement
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That he will give counsel in accordance with the laws]
καὶ τὸν βουλευτικὸν ὅρκον ὀμόσας, ἐν ᾧ ἦν κατὰ τοὺς νόμους βουλεύσειν
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: This passage in Xenophon refers to the trial, before the ekklesia, of the generals from the sea-battle at Arginusae who were charged with abandoning the bodies of the dead Athenians there. Socrates, who was the foreman (epistates) of the prytaneis who presided over the ekklesia, was the only one who opposed putting to the vote the illegal motion to condemn Thrasyllus and Erasinides and their colleagues to death as a single group, by a single vote. Acting in this way, against popular demand, and further incurring threats from powerful people, Socrates, Xenophon says, felt bound to honour his bouleutic oath rather than to humour the people's unjust request. Xenophon also discusses this trial in his Hellenica, see oath ids 2004 and 2005.
For further information on the bouleutic oath, see also remarks on Aristotle's Ath. Pol 22 (oath id 1775).
