  Remarks: The text indicates that "If they (whoever they may be?) should transgress their oath, the decision (as to their penalty?) is to rest with the hiaromaos at Olympia" (αἰ τὸ[ν] ὅρκον παρβαίνοιαν, γνῶμαν τὸρ ἰ[α]ρομάορ τ’ Ὀλυμπίαι).
Virgilio in SEG 38.364 suggested that the Anaitoi and the Metapioi, who are the parties to this treaty, were small communities of Elis located near Olympia. The hiaromaos was interpreted as a local official associated with the sacred altar to Zeus in the Altis at Olympia.
