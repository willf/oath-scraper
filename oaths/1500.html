Oath ID 1500: Herodotus, The Histories, 7.132, (literary, Hist., 428-415)


  Date: 480
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Delphi   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
The Greeks who fought against the Persians (male, any adult age, free, various)
  Swearee:
The Greeks who fought against the Persians (male, any adult age, free, various)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: unknown
  If refused: unknown
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: The Persian invasion was repelled
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That as many as were Greeks and surrendered to the Persians without having been compelled to do so, in good case (i.e. if they win) should be deprived of one tenth of their possessions for the god of Delphi]
ὅσοι τῷ Πέρσῃ ἔδοσαν σφέας αὐτοὺς Ἕλληνες ἐόντες, μὴ ἀναγκασθέντες, καταστάντων σφι εὖ τῶν πρηγμάτων, τούτους δεκατεῦσαι τῷ ἐν Δελφοῖσι θεῷ
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
Apollo?    
 
  Remarks: Herodotus states that after the Thessalians, Dolopes, Enienes, Perrhaebians, Locrians, Magnesians, Melians, Achaeans of the Phthia, Thebes and all Boeotia except Thespiae and Plataea had all paid tribute to Xerxes, all the Greeks who declared war against the barbarians entered into this sworn agreement.
The reference to "the god of Delphi" suggests that the oath was sworn by Apollo at Delphi, but Herodotus does not explicitly state this.
It seems that this oath was proceeded by a sworn alliance against the Persians (see oath id 1501), for this oath is presented as an afterthought: those who had levied war against the barbarian (aorist participle) swore that all Greeks who had surrendered to the Persians (aorist indicative) without being compelled should be, etc. In other words, the alliance was one thing, and this oath was a separate oath which happened later.
