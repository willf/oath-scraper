Oath ID 1979: Sophocles, Oedipus Tyrannus, 1086-1093, (literary, Trag., ?420s (date uncertain))


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Thebes   Location: Palace of Oedipus
 
  Swearer:
Chorus (male) (n/a, old, free, Theban)
  Swearee:
Cithaeron (n/a, n/a, n/a, n/a)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Highlights the chorus' false sense of security
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: [That, if the chorus is a seer and has wise judgement, Cithaeron will be honoured at the next full moon as mother and nurse and birth-place of Oedipus, with dancing]
εἴπερ ἐγὼ μάντις εἰ-
μι καὶ κατὰ γνώμαν ἴδρις,
οὐ τὸν Ὄλυμπον ἀπείρων,
ὦ Κιθαιρών, οὐκ ἔσῃ τὰν αὔριον
πανσέληνον μὴ οὐ σέ γε τὸν πατριώταν Οἰδίπου
καὶ τροφὸν καὶ ματέρ’ αὔξειν,
καὶ χορεύεσθαι
 
  Linguistic: ou + acc.
 
  God(s) invoked:
Olympos    
 
  Remarks: The oath is never fulfilled because the chorus is neither a seer nor wise in judgement. The informal oath formula οὐ + acc., current in Doric and Arcadian (cf. oath ids 575, 576, 1217, 1219, 1230, 2214, 2307), was not used in Attic, and in tragedy it is found only in Sophocles (see oath ids 244, 1978, 1980) and mostly, as here, in lyric.
