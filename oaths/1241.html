  Remarks: The peace treaty referred to here by the Chorus was originally proposed by Amphitheos at 51-52, envisaged as being between the Athenians and the Spartans. Dicaeopolis subsequently undertakes to make a thirty-year peace treaty with the Spartans alone (see oath id 596). Although a real peace treaty necessarily entails the swearing of an oath, Dicaeopolis never actually swears one in the context of this treaty. However, we must bear in mind that this is a comic treaty of a fantastical nature, exemplified by the fact that it is characterized by physical smells and flavours. But, we should also note that, at oath id 600, the Acharnians assume that the Spartans have sworn the treaty and that their oaths cannot be trusted. This treaty is referred to multiple times throughout the play.
