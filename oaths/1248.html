Oath ID 1248: Aristophanes, Wealth, 725, (literary, Comic., 388)


  Date: 388
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: In the assembly
 
  Swearer:
Neocleides (male, mature, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Athenian assembly (male, any adult age, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: The vote would be taken again
  If refused: The vote would not be taken again
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: The vote was taken again
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That the outcome of the vote had been wrongly judged]
 
  Linguistic: omnu
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Sommerstein comments ad loc.: "The term hupomosia was applied to various procedures by which an objection could be raised on oath which would obstruct or delay an Assembly decision or court case, but the reference here is to one of these procedures in particular. When the Assembly voted, as it normally did, by show of hands, its presiding officers estimated which side had the majority and announced their decision to the meeting. If any citizen asserted on oath that the outcome had been wrongly judged, the vote was taken againâ€¦. It would appear that Neocleides had been in the habit of challenging vote declarations in this way: Asclepius, by making him totally blind, wishes to make it impossible for him to do so, since no one who had not seen the hands go up could possibly swear that the vote had been incorrectly assessed". One possible inference from Xen. Hell. 1.7.34 (our only other source for this particular variety of hypomosia; see oath id 2004) might be that on any given motion the vote could only be repeated once.
