Oath ID 3664: Demosthenes, Against Timotheus, 66-67, (literary, Orat., 362?)


  Date: 386 to 362
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: the Pnyx
 
  Swearer:
Timotheus (male, mature, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Athenians in the assembly (male, any adult age, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: Destruction of self and dedication of property to sacred uses
 
  Taken: yes   True: no
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That he would indict Iphicrates as a usurper of citizen rights]
εἰ μὴ γράψοιτο Ἰφικράτην ξενίας
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork, wrk
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Apollodorus tells the jurors that it would be a long story to describe all the perjury of Timotheus, and relates one example, when Timotheus swore that he would indict Iphicrates as a usurper of citizen rights. According to Apollodorus, even though Timotheus swore this oath, and called down destruction upon himself if he failed to fulfil it, he later - to serve his own interests - gave his daugther in marriage to the son of Iphicrates.
Apollodorus asks the jurors whether it was strange that he refused to allow Timotheus to swear an oath to him (see oath id 3662) when he was not ashamed to deceive the demos, and does not fear the gods in whose name he has perjured himself.
It should be mentioned that no witnesses are called to prove that Timotheus ever said what is alleged; all we are given in the way of evidence is that old (un)reliable, "you all know".
