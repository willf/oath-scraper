  Statement: [That it was not Spithridates that asked him to suggest that Otys should marry Spithridates' daughter]
Μὰ τοὺς θεούς, ἔφη ὁ Ἀγησίλαος, ἐκεῖνος μὲν ἐμέ γε οὐκ ἐκέλευσε ταῦτα λέγειν·
 
  Linguistic: ma tous
 
  God(s) invoked:
The gods    
 
  Remarks: When Agesilaus suggested that Otys should marry his son to Spithridates' daughter, Otys asked whether the marriage proposal was acceptable to Spithridates.
Agesilaus responded, "by the gods, it was not he that asked me to say this".
Agesilaus had already suggested the marriage to Spithridates who expressed his willingness but doubted that Otys would agree. Spithridates had not empowered Agesilaus to make the offer on his behalf, nor had he forbidden it - he merely expressed doubt that it could happen. When Otys asked Agesilaus whether Spithridates was in favour of the match, Agesilaus said that Spithridates did not tell him to suggest the marriage, but that he was always delighted to think of ideas that would benefit his friends - in other words, it's my idea, not his, but I've reason to believe he'll have no objection. Otys then suggested that Agesilaus should find out whether Spithridates was in favour. Agesilaus then sent Herippidas to see Spithridates and the marriage was agreed.
