Oath ID 3736: Demosthenes, Against Olympiodorus, 25, (literary, Orat., 343/2)


  Date: 343
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:

  Swearee:
Pythodorus (the archon) (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown

  Remarks: Callistratus tells the jurors that when the Archon summoned into court all those who were filing claims according to the law, he entered a sworn statement (see oath id 3628), asking for a postponement on the ground that Olympiodorus was abroad on military service. However, their opponents swore a counter-hypomosia, and convinced the jurors that Olympiodorus had left Athens on account of the trial.
The exact mechanics of this procedure are unclear. If a hypomosia is submitted on behalf of one party, justice might seem obviously to demand that the presiding magistrate should then turn to the other party and ask him "Have you got anyone who is willing to swear the contrary?" If both parties swore, the jury had to vote, after hearing speeches, on the question which of them was telling the truth, and according to how they voted, either a postponement was allowed, or the case was decided against the absent party by default (see also oath id 3720).
