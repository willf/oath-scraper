Oath ID 3730: Demosthenes, Against Neaera, 78-9, (literary, Orat., 343-340)


  Date: 360 to 340
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: The sanctuary of Dionysus
 
  Swearer:
The Venerable Priestesses (female, old, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Wife of the archon basileus (female, mature/old, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Wife of the archon basileus
 
  If taken: They can hold office
  If refused: They cannot hold office
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features The presence of the altar
 
  Statement: [That they are pure, chaste, and without taint from things that are impure, including intercourse with men. That they celebrate the Feast of the Wine God and the Bacchic rite honouring the god in accordance with the ancestral rule and at the appointed times]
Ἁγιστεύω καὶ εἰμὶ καθαρὰ καὶ ἁγνὴ ἀπό <τε> τῶν ἄλλων τῶν οὐ καθαρευόντων καὶ ἀπ’ ἀνδρὸς συνουσίας, καὶ τὰ θεοίνια καὶ τὰ ἰοβάκχεια γεραρῶ τῷ Διονύσῳ κατὰ τὰ πάτρια καὶ ἐν τοῖς καθήκουσι χρόνοις.
 
  Linguistic: ork, wrk
 
  God(s) invoked:
Dionysus    
 
  Remarks: Apollodorus calls the sacred herald who waits upon the wife of the archon basileus when she administers the oath to the Venerable Priestesses (Gerarai) as they carry their baskets in front of the altar before they have contact with the rituals. He does so in order that the jurors might hear the oath and its wording (so far as this is permissible) and understand how holy and ancient the rites are.
After the oath has been read, Apollodorus reminds the jurors that they have heard the oath and the accepted rites handed down from their ancestors, and how Phano performed the rites and administered the oath to the Gerarai.
