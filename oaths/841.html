Oath ID 841: Aristophanes, Ecclesiazusae, 1026, (literary, Comic., Probably 391 (see Sommerstein))


  Date: Probably 391 (see Sommerstein) (391 to 390)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Outside Praxagora's house
 
  Swearer:
Epigenes (male, adolescent, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
World at large (n/a, n/a, unknown, n/a)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: Epigenes would not have to sleep with the old woman
  If refused: Epigenes will have to sleep with the old woman
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: no   True: n/a
  Impact: n/a
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That Epigenes has a legitimate reason for not sleeping with the old woman]
 
  Linguistic: wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: In an attempt to get out of having to sleep with the old woman, Epigenes suggests exwmosia, which is refused. This, as Sommerstein explains ad 1026, was "an oath declaring that one had a proper reason for failing to perform what would otherwise be one's legal duty; normally applied (1) to a witness swearing that he knew nothing of the facts about which he had been called to testify…. or (2) to a man elected to public office claiming that illness, pressures of work, etc., made it impossible for him to take the position… The allusion here is probably to (2)", though sources show (see Sommerstein ad loc. for references) that "to decline office in this way on any but medical grounds… was offensive to public sentiment, and the old woman's retort is in line with this attitude".
