Oath ID 3418: Demosthenes, Against Meidias, 198, (literary, Orat., 347/6)

  Statement: [That it was clear to him that - at the time when Meidias was going around alleging that Demosthenes had dropped the case - that some of those who are perfectly happy to talk to Meidias were sorry to hear it]
ἐμοὶ μὲν νὴ τὸν Δία καὶ τὸν Ἀπόλλω καὶ τὴν Ἀθηνᾶν (εἰρήσεται γάρ, εἴτ’ ἄμεινον εἴτε μή,) ὅθ’ οὗτος ὡς ἀπήλλαγμαι περιιὼν ἐλογοποίει, ἔνδηλοί τινες ἦσαν ἀχθόμενοι τῶν πάνυ τούτῳ λαλούντων ἡδέως.
 
  Linguistic: nh thn, nh ton
 
  God(s) invoked:

Athena    
 
  Remarks: MacDowell, Demosthenes: Against Meidias, (Oxford 1990), p46, writes that "an oath with two or three accusatives is used by D. to introduce a statement cautiously or apologetically". The context shows, at any rate, that the people he is talking about are people who are generally regarded as friends of Meidias, and that what he is claiming is that these people only act friendly towards him because he is influential.
