  Remarks: This entry takes into account textual changes in SEG 30.406.
This inscription was found at Olympia and outlines the arrangement of the affairs of the settlement of Scillus by the Eleans.
The rhetra outlines the appointment of two officials (the damiorgoi) Nicarchidas and Pleistaenus and their duties, one of which is to oversee the prosecution of anyone who causes stasis. The damiorgoi were to put them on trial, and choose men (presumably to act as jurors). Before taking office these men would swear an oath by the god of Olympus i.e. Zeus Olympios. If they refused to swear the oath, they would pay a fine of 5 mnas each day for sacrifices to Zeus Olympios. The text of lines 9-12 is as follows: ὀμόσαντες πο<τ> τὸν θεὸν τὸν Ὀλύν[πιον· αἰ δέ τις μὲ ὀμόσαιτο, πέντε μνᾶ]ς κ’ ἀποτίνοι τᾶς ἀμέρας κατ(τ)θυτὰς τοῖ Δὶ Ὀλυ[νπίοι·].
