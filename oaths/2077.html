Oath ID 2077: Xenophon, Anabasis, 2.1.21-3, (literary, Hist., ca.370-355)


  Date: 401
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Persia   Location: near Cunaxa
 
  Swearer:
Clearchus (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Phalinus (male, mature/old, power-holder, unknown)
 
  Proposed by:
Artaxerxes II
 
  If taken: The truce would be formalised
  If refused: The Persians will attack the Greeks
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: no   True: n/a
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That his army would remain where it was]
 
  Linguistic: spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: After the battle of Cunaxa, King Artaxerxes sent Phalinus to negotiate with the Greeks regarding a truce.
Phalinus tells Clearchus the Greek commander that Artaxerxes has said that if the Greeks remain where they are there will be a truce. If they advance or retire, there will be war.
When asked for his answer, Clearchus responds that his view is the same as that of the King.
When asked to clarify, Clearchus replies, "if we remain a truce, if we retire or advance, war".
Phalinus again asks Clearchus whether he should report a truce or war, and Clearchus again makes the same reply, but does not indicate what he wishes to do. The truce proposed here is referred to also by Ctesias (see oath id 3477).
