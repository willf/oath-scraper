Oath ID 431: IG II(2), 111, 17-23, (inscriptional, Public, 363/2)


  Date: 376-375 or ca.364/3 (376 to 363)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Ceos   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Chabrias (male, mature, power-holder, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Cean)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: Their alliance would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be an alliance
  If kept: Many good things (?)
  If broken: ill (?)
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would not harbour grudges for what is past against any of the Ceans, nor would they kill or make an exile any of the Ceans who abide by the oaths and this agreement, but they would bring them into the alliance like the other allies. But if any one commits an act of revolution in Ceos contrary to the oaths and the agreement, they would not allow him by any craft or contrivance as far as possible. If any one does not wish to live in Ceos, they would allow him to live wherever he wishes in the allied cities and enjoy his own property]
 
  Linguistic: ork, wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: This inscription refers to an alliance between Athens and the poleis on Ceos.
The text notes that Chabrias swore on behalf of the Athenians and those Ceans whom the Athenians reinstated. The exact statement is unknown. However, it must have been similar to the new agreement sworn by the board of strategoi in 363/2 (see oath id 422)
For a full analysis of the text see Rhodes and Osborne, GHI 39.
See also oath id 422, 424, 427, 520, 521.
