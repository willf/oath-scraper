Oath ID 1308: Hesiod, Catalogue of Women, fr. 204.78-84, (literary, Epic., 580-520)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: Tyndareus' palace
 
  Swearer:
Helen's suitors (male, adolescent/mature, free, Greek)
  Swearee:
Tyndareus (male, old, power-holder, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
Tyndareus
 
  If taken: They will be considered as a suitor
  If refused: They will not be considered as a suitor
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: Trojan War
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features libation
 
  Statement: [That none of them would attempt to make Helen his wife without Tyndareus' consent, and they each would attack anyone who attempted to do so]

  Remarks: Most of the suitors remain true to their oath, but Odysseus attempts to wriggle out of the second clause of the oath by pretending to be mad. Ultimately however, the suitors join forces to attack Paris, the man who took Helen as wife without Tyndareus' consent.
