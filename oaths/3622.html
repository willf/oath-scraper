Oath ID 3622: Theopompus, Philippica, Fr. 104.3 Jacoby, (literary, Hist., 324-322)


  Date: 478
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Delos   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Aristides (male, mature, power-holder, Athenian)
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Chian)
Representatives of various poleis (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
  Swearee:
Aristides (male, mature, power-holder, Athenian)
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Chian)
Representatives of various poleis (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
 
  Proposed by:
Aristides
 
  If taken: Their alliance would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be an alliance
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: Delian League formed
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would have the same enemies and friends, and they would remain loyal and not desert (the Athenian masses or the allies of the Athenians)]
 
  Linguistic: summac
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: In spite of the alliance, there were frequent quarrels amongst the allies. For other references to the Delian League see oath id 1778 with cross references. Here Theopompus refers to Athens having adopted a practice of adding 'and for the Chians' to all official prayers for the city's prosperity. We have more direct evidence for this practice in Aristophanes, Birds 879-880. In fact, this Theopompus fragment comes from a scholium on that passage. Chios had a special position in the Athenian alliance because, after the Lesbian revolt of 428, it was the only member of the League (except for the unimportant Methymna) which did not pay tribute. See also Thrasymachus fr. 3 D-K.
