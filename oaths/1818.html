Oath ID 1818: Thucydides, History, 8.57.2-59.1, (literary, Hist., 403-395)


  Date: 412/11 (412)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Persia   Location: Plain of Maeander
 
  Swearer:
Tissaphernes (male, mature/old, power-holder, Persian)
Representatives (male, any adult age, power-holder, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Tissaphernes (male, mature/old, power-holder, Persian)
Representatives (male, any adult age, power-holder, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: The treaty would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a treaty
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following terms:
1. The King's country in Asia is to remain his possession and to be governed as he pleases
2. The Spartans and their allies shall not go against the country of the King, nor the King against the Spartans and their allies.
3. If any of the Spartans and their allies go against the King's territory, the Spartans and their allies shall stop them, and if any of the King's subjects go against the Spartans and their allies the King shall stop them
4. Tissaphernes shall pay for the ships now present until the arrival of the King's ships. After these arrive, the Spartans can then pay for their own ships if they wish. If not, Tissaphernes will provide money, but the Spartans will repay the money at the end of the war
5. When the King's ships arrive, the ships of the Spartans and their allies and those of the King shall wage war together, according to what seems best to Tissaphernes and the Spartans and their allies. If they wish to end the war with the Athenians, they will do so jointly]
 
  Linguistic: spend, spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Thucydides states that after this agreement was made Tissaphernes set about preparing to bring Phoenician ships as had been agreed.
The role played by Hieramenes and the sons of Pharnaces who are named in the agreement is unclear. Gomme, Andrewes and Dover, A Historical Commentary on Thucydides, Volume V, p139, argue that Krumbholz was correct to see Hieramenes (also mention in Xen. Hell. 2.1.9) and "the sons of Pharnaces" as witnesses to the treaty rather than the highest Persian officers in the area. This seems plausible since it is unlikely that the term "the sons of Pharnaces" is intended to include Pharnabazus who was a satrap in his own right.
For references to the other agreements between the Persians and the Spartans see also oath id 1806.
