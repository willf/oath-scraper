Oath ID 1272: Herodotus, The Histories, 1.65, (literary, Hist., 428-415)


  Date: During the lifetime of Lycurgus (900 to 775)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
A Spartan youth (male, adolescent, free, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Other members of the sworn band (male, any adult age, free, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
Lycurgus
 
  If taken: He could be part of a sworn band
  If refused: He could not be part of a sworn band
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That he would fight while he was alive, and would not regard being alive as more important than being free; that he would not leave his taxiarch or his enomotarch, whether he was alive or dead, and that he would bury the dead among his fellow-fighters, and that he would leave no-one unburied]
 
  Linguistic: wmot
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Herodotus states that Lycurgus established all that related to war at Sparta: the "sworn companies" (the enomotiai), the bands of thirty, and the common messes.
The date (and the very existence) of Lycurgus is open to debate. Herodotus names Lycurgus as the guardian of the Agiad king Leobotes (ca.900), but most later writers associate him with the Eurypontid king Charillos (ca.775).
However, it seems most likely that the reforms to the Spartan constitution took place much later during the sixth century.
For this oath see Hans van Wees, "The Oath of the Sworn Bands, the Acharnae stele, the Oath of Plataea and archaic Spartan warfare", in A. Luther. M. Meier, & L. Thommen (eds.), Das Fruhe Sparta, Franz Steiner, Stuttgart 2006, pp.125-64.
