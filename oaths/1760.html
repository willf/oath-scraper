  Remarks: Thucydides notes that one of the terms of the peace treaty between Argos and Sparta (see oath id 1746) included a clause regarding the offering to Apollo Pythaeus .
Thucydides states at 5.53 that the Epidaurians were obliged to send a sacrifice to the Argive-controlled sanctuary of Apollo Pythaeus as payment for pasturage. The Argives accused the Epidaurians of not doing so, and war broke out.
Thucydides adds that the Argives did so on the advice of Alcibiades. Both parties hoped to bring Epidaurus into the "Argive alliance" in order to keep Corinth quiet, and to reduce the time it would take to bring aid to Argos from Athens by sea.
The clause in the treaty between Sparta and Argos stipulated that the Argives "if they wished, might tender an oath to the Epidaurians, or, if not, should swear one themselves". This appears to mean that the dispute over the offering should be settled by the procedure of oath-challenge, with the Argives having the right to choose whether to demand an oath from the Epidaurians (presumably to the effect that they did not owe anything to the sanctuary) or to swear one themselves (presumably to the contrary effect); the side refusing to swear would be deemed to have accepted that it was in the wrong.
See also oath id 1747.
