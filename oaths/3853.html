Oath ID 3853: Xenophon, Anabasis, 3.2.2, (literary, Hist., ca.370-355)


  Date: 401
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Persia   Location: near Cunaxa
 
  Swearer:
Ariaeus (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
Highest Persian officers (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
  Swearee:
Greek generals (male, mature, power-holder, various)
Greek lochagoi (male, mature, power-holder, various)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: Their alliance would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be an alliance
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features The sacrifice of a bull, a wolf, a boar and a ram over a shield; the Greeks dipped a spear into the blood, and the Persians dipped a spear
 
  Statement: [That they would be allies to the Greeks, would not betray them, and would lead them without treachery]
 
  Linguistic: summac
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Xenophon refers here to the alliance between the Ten Thousand and the Persians who fought with Cyrus.
After the Greeks elected new leaders, Cheirisophus the Spartan addressed the Greek mercenaries saying that their present situation was particularly painful because they had been robbed of their generals and been betrayed by Ariaeus and his men, "who were formerly allies".
See also oath id 2169.
