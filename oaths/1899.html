Oath ID 1899: Xenophon, Hellenica, 2.4.30, (literary, Hist., 385-354)


  Date: 404
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: Sellasia
 
  Swearer:
Envoys (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Representatives of the Spartans and their allies (male, any adult age, free, various)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: Peace treaty formalised
  If refused: Hostilities will continue
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following terms:
1. The Athenians were to demolish the Long Walls and the Piraeus wall
2. The Athenians were to surrender all but twelve of their ships
3. The Athenians were to receive back their exiles
4. The Athenians were to have the same enemies and friends as the Spartans
5. The Athenians were to follow the Spartans where they might lead on land and sea]
 
  Linguistic: spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Xenophon states that when King Pausanias marched on the Piraeus, he was accompanied by all the allies except the Corinthians and Boeotians who said that they would not be keeping their oaths if they made a military expedition against the Athenians when they were not doing anything against the treaty.
For other references to this peace treaty see oath id 1893.
