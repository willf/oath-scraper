Oath ID 1628: Thucydides, History, 3.64.3, (literary, Hist., 403-395)


  Date: 480
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Delphi   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
The Greeks who fought against the Persians (male, any adult age, free, various)
  Swearee:
The Greeks who fought against the Persians (male, any adult age, free, various)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: They would be included in the alliance
  If refused: They would not
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: The Persian invasion was repelled
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would fight together against the Persians to maintain Greek liberty]
 
  Linguistic: omos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The Theban envoy again accuses the Plataeans of having betrayed the Greeks with whom they had sworn oaths against the Persians.
The Theban envoy blurs the distinction between the oaths sworn against the Persians and the oaths sworn after the battle of Plataea (see oath id 1598), suggesting that the Plataeans are arguing that the oath sworn against the Persians should protect them now - which they are not. See also oath id 1501.
