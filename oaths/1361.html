Oath ID 1361: Anonymus Homericus, Hymn to Hermes, 514-523, (literary, Hymn., fifth century)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Pylos   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Hermes (male, child, power-holder, n/a)
  Swearee:
Apollo (male, mature, power-holder, n/a)
 
  Proposed by:
Apollo
 
  If taken: Apollo will be pleased
  If refused: Apollo will only be pleased if Hermes makes the promise
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: no   True: n/a
  Impact: n/a
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That he will not steal Apollo's lyre or bow]
Δείδια Μαιάδος υἱὲ διάκτορε ποικιλομῆτα
μή μοι ἀνακλέψῃς κίθαριν καὶ καμπύλα τόξα·
τιμὴν γὰρ πὰρ Ζηνὸς ἔχεις ἐπαμοίβιμα ἔργα
θήσειν ἀνθρώποισι κατὰ χθόνα πουλυβότειραν.
ἀλλ’ εἴ μοι τλαίης γε θεῶν μέγαν ὅρκον ὀμόσσαι,
ἢ κεφαλῇ νεύσας ἢ ἐπὶ Στυγὸς ὄβριμον ὕδωρ,
πάντ’ ἂν ἐμῷ θυμῷ κεχαρισμένα καὶ φίλα ἔρδοις.
Καὶ τότε Μαιάδος υἱὸς ὑποσχόμενος κατένευσε
μή ποτ’ ἀποκλέψειν ὅσ’ Ἑκηβόλος ἐκτεάτισται,
μηδέ ποτ’ ἐμπελάσειν πυκινῷ δόμῳ·
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
Water of Styx    
 
  Remarks: Apollo proposes that Hermes should swear "the great oath of the gods, either by nodding his head, or upon the dread water of the Styx" that he will not steal Apollo's lyre or bow. It seems that since Hermes and Apollo are now friends (cf. 507), Apollo feels he cannot really demand an oath, although he would like to, and so gives Hermes the alternative of nodding in assent. Hermes once more PROMISES rather than swears (cf. oath id 1358) and chooses to nod his head, a much weaker sign of confirmation than the god's oath on Styx. But, he promises more than Apollo had asked for by agreeing never to steal Apollo's property nor to come near his strong room. In return for this promise, Apollo agrees that Hermes will be his greatest friend among both gods and mortals. Furthermore, he rewards Hermes with a gold wand of wealth and fortune to keep him safe.
