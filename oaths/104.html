  Remarks: Graffiti on underside of base fragment of a black-glazed pyxis.
Lang's text from the editio princeps is very fragmentary. All we can say for certain is that the author swore by Zeus and Apollo and the other gods. Lang writes, "The oath on the first line of (a) may have included one or two other deities; it must have been followed by a wish that a certain person might be avenged or punished".
Oikonomides suggested an alternate text in SEG 36.135. This version can be considered unreliable on epigraphic grounds. His text reads as follows:
[- - - ὤμοσ]ε τὸν Δία καὶ τὸν Ἀσ̣[κληπιὸν - - -]
[- - - δίκην, ὦ θ]ε̣οί, τείσαιτο vacat
καὶ τὸν [αἰ]νετὸ[ν] θεὸ[ν]. Oikonomides translates the text as follows: "he took an oath by Zeus and Asclepius...(I hope), Oh Gods, that he will have to pay for it the right price. And (I trust) the praiseworthy God (will hear me?).
