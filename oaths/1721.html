  Statement: [That they would abide by this agreement and this treaty, justly and guilelessly]
ἐμμενῶ ταῖς ξυνθήκαις καὶ ταῖς σπονδαῖς ταῖσδε δικαίως καὶ ἀδόλως
The terms of the agreement can be summarised as follows:
1. Free access to common sanctuaries
2. The independence of Delphi
3. Peace to last on land and sea for fifty years
4. It was to be unlawful for either the Spartans and their allies or the Athenians and their allies to bear arms against each other (they were to have recourse to courts and oaths if either party violated this rule)
5. Amphipolis was to be restored to Athens
6. In the case of other cities delivered to Athens by the Spartans (Argilus, Stagirus, Acanthus, Stolus, Olynthus and Spartolus) were to be safeguarded if they paid the tribute fixed in the time of Aristeides
7. The Mecybernaeans and Sanaeans and Singians should dwell in their own towns on the same terms as the Olynthians and Acanthians
8. The Spartans were to restore Panactum to the Athenians and the Athenians were to restore Coryphasium, Cythera, Methana, Pteleum and Atalante and any Spartan captives to the Spartans, and any captured Peloponnesians to their homelands
9. The fate of Scione, Torone, Sermyle and any other city held by the Athenians was to be decided by the Athenians as they saw fit.

  Remarks: Nicias proposed sending an embassy to Sparta to urge them to restore Panactum to the Athenians intact and to give up the alliance with the Boeotians unless they should accede to "the treaty" i.e. the peace of Nicias
