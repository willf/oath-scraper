Oath ID 2256: Xenophon, Agesilaus, 1.10-12, (literary, Biogr., 360-355)


  Date: 396
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Ephesus   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Agesilaus (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Tissaphernes (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
 
  Proposed by:
Tissaphernes
 
  If taken: The truce would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a truce
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That he would observe the truce guilelessly, allowing three months for the transaction]
σπονδὰς ἄξειν ἀδόλως, ὁρισάμενος τῆς πράξεως τρεῖς μῆνας.
 
  Linguistic: ork, spond, wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: According to Xenophon, Tissaphernes approached Agesilaus regarding a truce and swore that he would do his best to convince the King to guarantee the autonomy of the Greeks in Asia if Agesilaus agreed to halt hostilities (see oath id 2255).
Agesilaus swore to abide by the truce for three months.
Xenophon states that whereas Tissaphernes broke his oath at once, Agesilaus continued to keep the truce even though he was aware that Tissaphernes had broken his oath.
Thus, by showing Tissaphernes to be a perjurer Agesilaus made him distrusted everywhere, and by proving himself to be true to his oaths and his agreements, Agesilaus encouraged all - both Greeks and barbarians - to make an agreement with him whenever he wished.
Xenophon also describes this oath exchange in the Hellenica (see oath id 1916-7).
