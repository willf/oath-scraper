  Statement: [That Aristocrates should not have added the clause 'he shall be liable to seizure' after naming an accusation]
μὴ μὰ Δί’ αὐτὸ τὸ τῆς αἰτίας ὄνομ’ εἰπόντα ‘ἀγώγιμος ἔστω’ προσγράψαι.
 
  Linguistic: ma

Zeus    
 
  Remarks: Euthycles directs his comments to Aristocrates himself rather than the jurors.
The point being made is that if Aristocrates was going to create a power of summary arrest, he should not have attached to it the bare, unqualified condition "if anyone kills Charidemus" but should have specified e.g. whether the killing had to be intentional, whether it had to be unjustifiable, and whether the killer had to be a citizen. The speaker goes on to argue (49-50) that the decree, as it stands, would in effect make it possible to get someone extradited for an act that wasn’t even a crime (e.g. a killing in self-defence).
