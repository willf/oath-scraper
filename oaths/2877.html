Oath ID 2877: Lysias, For Polystratus, 26, (literary, Orat., 410-403/2)


  Date: 413
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Catana   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Catanian recruits (male, any adult age, free, Catanaean)
  Swearee:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Syracusan)
 
  Proposed by:
The Syracusan
 
  If taken: They can join the Syracusan army
  If refused: They cannot join the Syracusan army
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: n/a   True:
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would obey the orders of their officers and the Syracusan people]
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The speaker is trying to prove his loyalty to Athens and tells the jurors that when he was serving in Catana, a Syracusan arrived with an oath (i.e. to recruit Catanians for the Syracusan army).
According to the speaker, the Syracusan was ready to make people swear the oath, and was going individually to each person present. The speaker spoke against him, and went to a certain Tydeus and explained the situation to him. (Tydeus - later a general; he perished in or after the battle of Aegospotami, cf. Xen. Hell. 2.1.16, 26 - was probably a son of the famous Lamachus; see CQ 28 (1978) 383.)
The speaker does not say whether his initiative succeeded in stopping the recruitment drive, and his silence strongly suggests that it did not.
