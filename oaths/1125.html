Oath ID 1125: IC IV, 72, Col III, ll 44-52, (inscriptional, Public, about 450)


  Date: About 450 (460 to 440)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Gortyn   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Relatives (male, any adult age, free, Gortynian)
Witnesses (male, any adult age, free, Gortynian)
  Swearee:
unknown (n/a, unknown, unknown, unknown)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: They will be believed
  If refused: They will not be believed
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: n/a   True:
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they had brought the child to the husband, and that he rejected it]
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: According to the text, if a wife who is divorced gives birth to a child the relatives are to bring the child to her ex-husband in the presence of witnesses. If the man rejects his child, the child will be in the mother's power either to rear or expose. The text indicates that the relatives and witnesses are to be believed (horkioteroi) if they swear that the child was brought to the man.
See also oath id 3868.
For the regulation concerning children of serfs see oath id 1127.
