Oath ID 2583: Aeschines, On the Embassy, 82-5, (literary, Orat., 343)


  Date: 346
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Critobulus of Lampsacus (male, mature, power-holder, Lampsacene)
  Swearee:
Envoys (male, mature/old, power-holder, Macedonian)
 
  Proposed by:
Philocrates
 
  If taken: Cersobleptes would be included in the peace
  If refused: He would not be included in the peace
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: no   True:
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following terms:
1. Both parties recognised the territory of the other;
2. There was to be an alliance and peace between Philip and the Athenians, with no time limit;
3. The peace and the alliance was to be binding to the allies of both parties;
4. The seas were to be kept safe i.e. neither party was to support pirates]
 
  Linguistic: eirhn, ork, spend
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: When defending himself against Demosthenes' allegations that he is responsible for King Cersobleptes losing his kingdom, Aeschines states that on the day the Athenians voted to conclude the peace Demosthenes did not mention Cersobleptes. He states that when the ambassadors had been elected to receive the oaths from Philip but had not yet set out, there was an assembly at which Demosthenes was one of the proedroi. Critobulus of Lampsacus addressed this assembly and said that Cersobleptes had sent him, and demanded that he should be allowed to give his oath to Philip's ambassadors, and that Cersobleptes' name should be written down in the list of the allies of Athens.
Aleximachus of Peleces submitted a motion to the proedroi to the effect that the representative of Cersobleptes should be permitted to join the other allies in giving the oath to Philip, but Demosthenes (in his capacity as one of the proedroi) refused to put the motion to the vote.
Demosthenes argued that he would not destroy the peace with Philip, and that he did not recognise allies who joined only in time to pour the peace libations.
See also oath id 2585.
For other references to the Peace of Philocrates see also oath id 2517.
