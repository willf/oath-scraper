Oath ID 962: Rhodes and Osborne, GHI, 78, lines 26-35, (inscriptional, Public, ca.337)

  Remarks: This text is part of a trilingual inscription (Lycian and Greek on the front and Aramaic on the side) which records a resolution by the Xanthians and their perioikoi.
The Xanthians and their perioikoi swore to do completely what was inscribed on the stele i.e. to establish an altar to the Caunian King and Arkesimas, to appoint Simias the son of Kondorasis as priest (and his descendants for all time), to give Simias ateleia, land, a salary of 1.5 minas, to make new freedmen pay 2 drachmas to the god, and to ensure that the sacrifices are made to the Caunian King and Arkesimas.
Presumably the Caunian King, Arkesimas, Leto, the descendants of Leto i.e. Apollo and Artemis, and the Nymphs were invoked as part of the oath since it is stated that anyone violates the oath he will be a sinner against them.
The text indicates that Pixodarus is to have authority.
