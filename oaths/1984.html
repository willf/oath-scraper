Oath ID 1984: Xenophon, Hellenica, 6.5.2-3, (literary, Hist., 385-354)

  Statement: [That they will abide by the treaty which the King sent down, and by the decrees of the Athenians and their allies. And if anyone takes the field against any of the cities which have sworn this oath, they shall help with all their strength]
Ἐμμενῶ ταῖς σπονδαῖς ἃς βασιλεὺς κατέπεμψε καὶ τοῖς ψηφίσμασι τοῖς Ἀθηναίων καὶ τῶν συμμάχων. ἐὰν δέ τις στρατεύῃ ἐπί τινα πόλιν τῶν ὀμοσασῶν τόνδε τὸν ὅρκον, βοηθήσω παντὶ σθένει
 
  Linguistic: eirhn, omos, ork, spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Xenophon states that the Athenians invited to Athens all the poleis which wished to participate in the peace which the King had sent down i.e. the King's Peace of 387/6.
When they came the Athenians passed a decree to take the following oath: "I will abide by the treaty which the King sent down, and by the decrees of the Athenians and their allies. And if anyone takes the field against any of the cities which have sworn this oath, I shall help with all my strength".
Xenophon states that all the others were pleased with the oath except for the Eleans, who said that it was not right to make the Marganians, Scilluntians or Triphylians autonomous, because these cities belonged to them.
Despite their objections, the Athenians and their allies, after voting that both small and great cities should be autonomous alike, just as the King wrote, sent out the oath-commisioners (horkotai) and directed them to administer the oath to the highest officials in each city.
Xenophon observes that they all took the oath except for the Eleans.
The Mantineans now felt that they were entirely independent, and voted to make Mantinea a single city and to build a wall around it.
For references to the original King's Peace see oath id 328.
For other references to the Common Peace of 371 see oath id 1974, 1975, 1976, 1986, 1987, 1988, 1989, 1991, 1992, 3426, 3716.
