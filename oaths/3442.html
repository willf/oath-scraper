Oath ID 3442: Demosthenes, Against Aristocrates, 96-7, (literary, Orat., 352)


  Date: 352
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Hill of Ardettus (?)
 
  Swearer:
Dikastai (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Presiding magistrate (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Presiding magistrate
 
  If taken: They can be jurors
  If refused: They cannot be jurors
  If kept: Many blessings (?)
  If broken: Destruction of self and household (?)
 
  Taken: no   True:
  Impact: n/a
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they will vote according to the laws and decrees of the Athenian people and the Council of Five Hundred; <and concerning matters about which there are no laws, they will judge according to their perfectly honest opinion, and not from favour nor from enmity>. Nor will they accept gifts for judging, nor will any other man or woman do so on their behalf with their knowledge. And they will give an equal hearing to the accuser and the defendant alike, and cast their vote in relation to the actual matter in issue.]
 
  Linguistic: omwm, ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
Zeus    
Poseidon    
Demeter    
 
  Remarks: Euthycles states that the jurors have sworn "to judge according to their perfectly honest opinion" (γνώμῃ τῇ δικαιοτάτῃ δικάσειν).
He argues that if the jurors decide in favour of the defendant in a graphe paranomon because the prosecutors failed to make their case due to collusion or incompetence, their decision will result in the continuation in force of an illegal decree, but is nevertheless in keeping with their oath to judge according to their perfectly honest opinion. He argues that this is so because the view that commends itself to their judgement is guided by the speeches they hear, and if they vote in accordance with that view they vote piously. He goes on to argue that every man who does not vote against his better judgement due to spite or favour or another dishonest motive votes piously.
For the details of the Heliastic oath see oath id 3494.
