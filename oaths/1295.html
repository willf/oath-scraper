  Remarks: Like the references at ids 1291 and 1293, this reference concerns the Styx as "oath" in the sense that she is invoked by gods to guarantee their oaths. In this passage, Hesiod lists the punishments for any god who swears a false oath on the river Styx. For one year, he is laid flat and does not breathe; nor is he allowed come near ambrosia and nectar to eat, but without breath and without voice he lies on a bed and the evil coma covers him. After this year of punishment, the perjuring god is then cut off from all share in the life of the everlasting gods for nine years, and has no part in their counsels or festivals. In the tenth year, he mingles once more in the assemblies of gods on Olympus.
