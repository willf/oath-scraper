Oath ID 2885: Isocrates, Concerning the team of horses, 2, (literary, Orat., 397)


  Date: 397
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Prosecutors (male, any adult age, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Presiding magistrate (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Presiding magistrate
 
  If taken: They could prosecute
  If refused: The case cannot proceed
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That the facts pleaded in their written statement were true]
 
  Linguistic: wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The defendant - Alcibiades the younger - argues that his opponents are spending more time slandering his father than they are in informing the jurors about their sworn charges (antomosia).
This passage refers not just to the present prosecution but to a whole series of charges that have been brought against the speaker, not all of which involve alleged thefts. In the present case there can have been no allegation "that the defendant had stolen", since the alleged theft had been committed by his father when he himself was a small child; rather, the prosecutor is suing him for the value of the property of which he alleges he was wrongly deprived.
