Oath ID 2540: Plato, Republic, 379e3, (literary, Phil., 380-370)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Troy   Location: On the battle field
 
  Swearer:
Greek army (male, adolescent/mature, free, various Greek)
Trojan army (male, adolescent/mature, free, Trojan)
  Swearee:
Greek army (male, adolescent/mature, free, various Greek)
Trojan army (male, adolescent/mature, free, Trojan)
 
  Proposed by:
Hector
 
  If taken: War will be determined by single combat between Paris and Menelaus
  If refused: War will continue
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: Let their brains be spilled on the ground and those of their sons, and let their wives be the spoils of others
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: peace
  Consequences of breach: war resumes
 
  Sanctifying features Sheep sacrificed, wine poured, hands washed with water, fruit brought
 
  Statement: [That the Greeks and the Trojans will stop fighting, and that Menelaus and Paris will engage in single combat over Helen and her possessions]
 
  Linguistic: ork, spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
Zeus    
Helios    
Rivers    
Gaia    
Nether Avengers against dead perjurers    
All the immortal gods    
 
  Remarks: This is a reference to the oaths of truce sworn between the Greeks and the Trojans as recounted in Homer (see oath id 316 with further references). In the Republic, the fact that the violation is presented as being brought about by Athena and Zeus makes the story unacceptable material for the members of the ideal state.
