Oath ID 1734: Thucydides, History, 5.57.1-2, (literary, Hist., 403-395)


  Date: Winter of 419/8 (419)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Corinthian)
  Swearee:
Representatives (male, any adult age, power-holder, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: Their alliance would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be an alliance
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would have the same friends and enemies as the Spartans, and follow the Spartans whithersoever they may lead]
 
  Linguistic: summac
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The Corinthians seem to have rejoined the Peloponnesian League in the winter of 419/8, for Thucydides includes them in a list of the allies of Sparta.
The Corinthians were still part of the Argive alliance in 420/19 because Thucydides (5.55) mentions that they attended a conference of Argos' allies at Mantinea that year. They are not mentioned again until the military campaign in 418/7.
For other references to the so-called Peloponnesian League see oath id 1517.
