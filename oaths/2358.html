Oath ID 2358: Andocides, On the Mysteries, 97-8, (literary, Orat., 400/399)


  Date: 410/409 (410)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
All Athenians (male, any adult age, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
All Athenians (male, any adult age, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Demophantus
 
  If taken: They can be citizens
  If refused: They cannot be citizens
  If kept: many blessings
  If broken: Destruction of self and family
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unblemished sacrifices
 
  Statement: [That they would kill, by word and deed and vote and their own hand, if they could, anyone who overthrows the democracy at Athens and anyone who holds any office in the future after democracy has been overthrown, and anyone who sets himself up to be tyrant or helps set up a tyrant. And if anyone else kills the man, they would consider him pure in the eyes of both gods and spirits, because he has killed an enemy of the Athenians, and they would sell all the property of the dead man and give half to the killer, and would not hold any back. And if anyone dies in killing or attempting to kill any such man, they would care for both him and his children, just as for Harmodius and Aristogeiton and their descendants. And they declare null and void all oaths that have been sworn at Athens or in the camp or anywhere else against the people of Athens]
κτενῶ καὶ λόγῳ καὶ ἔργῳ καὶ ψήφῳ καὶ τῇ ἐμαυτοῦ χειρί, ἂν δυνατὸς ὦ, ὃς ἂν καταλύσῃ τὴν δημοκρατίαν τὴν Ἀθήνησι. καὶ ἐάν τις ἄρξῃ τιν’ ἀρχὴν καταλελυμένης τῆς δημοκρατίας τὸ λοιπόν, καὶ ἐάν τις τυραννεῖν ἐπαναστῇ ἢ τὸν τύραννον συγκαταστήσῃ. Καὶ ἐάν τις ἄλλος ἀποκτείνῃ, ὅσιον αὐτὸν νομιῶ εἶναι καὶ πρὸς θεῶν καὶ δαιμόνων, ὡς πολέμιον κτείναντα τὸν Ἀθηναίων, καὶ τὰ κτήματα τοῦ ἀποθανόντος πάντα ἀποδόμενος ἀποδώσω τὰ ἡμίσεα τῷ ἀποκτείναντι, καὶ οὐκ ἀποστερήσω οὐδέν. Ἐὰν δέ τις κτείνων τινὰ τούτων ἀποθάνῃ ἢ ἐπιχειρῶν, εὖ ποιήσω αὐτόν τε καὶ τοὺς παῖδας τοὺς ἐκείνου καθάπερ Ἁρμόδιόν τε καὶ Ἀριστογείτονα καὶ τοὺς ἀπογόνους αὐτῶν. δὲ ὅρκοι ὀμώμονται Ἀθήνησιν ἢ ἐν τῷ στρατοπέδῳ ἢ ἄλλοθί που ἐναντίοι τῷ δήμῳ τῷ Ἀθηναίων, λύω καὶ ἀφίημι.
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Andocides cites the decree proposed by Demophantos in 410/409. The decree stipulated that all Athenians were to swear over unblemished sacrifices to kill any man who overthrows the democracy at Athens or holds office after democracy has been overthrown (See also oath id 2748, 2749, 2750).
The decree states that the oath was to be taken before the Dionysia, in the customary manner.
Andocides argues that this law has now become invalid - otherwise Epichares, one of his prosecutors, having served on the boule under the Thirty, could have been killed with impunity - along with all laws older than the archonship of Eucleides (403/2) which have not been promulgated as part of the revised law-code.
See also oath id 3383.
