Oath ID 279: IG I(3), 14, 21-45, (inscriptional, Public, 460s or 453/2)


  Date: 460s or 453/2 (469 to 452)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Erythrae   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Bouleutai (male, mature, free, Erythraean)
  Swearee:
Oath-commissioners? (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Lysicrates?
 
  If taken: unknown
  If refused: unknown
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: Destruction of self and children
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features burning sacrifices
 
  Statement: [That they would deliberate as best they could for the Erythraean demos and the Athenians and their allies, and they would not rebel against the Athenians or their allies, either on their own initiative nor persuaded by anyone else, nor would they receive back any exiles; of those who fled to the Medes, without the permission of the Athenians and the demos, they would not banish those who remain in Erythrae without the permission of the Athenians and the demos; if any Erythraean kills another Erythraean he shall be put to death if he is judged guilty; if banishment is the sentence he shall be exiled from the Athenian alliance and his property shall be confiscated; if someone is caught betraying the city to tyrants he shall be put to death, both he and his children unless they report him to the demos; his children shall receive half his property and the rest shall be confiscated..]
