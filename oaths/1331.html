Oath ID 1331: Herodotus, The Histories, 4.201, (literary, Hist., 428-415)


  Date: During the reign of Darius ca.520-510 (520 to 510)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Libya   Location: Barca
 
  Swearer:
Amasis the Persian general (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
Rulers (male, mature, power-holder, Barcaean)
  Swearee:
Amasis the Persian general (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
Rulers (male, mature, power-holder, Barcaean)
 
  Proposed by:
Amasis?
 
  If taken: Peace treaty formalised
  If refused: Hostilities will continue
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: The Barcaeans opened their gates and allowed the Persians to enter their city
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That their treaty should hold good while the ground where they stood was unchanged]
ἔστ’ ἂν ἡ γῆ αὕτη οὕτω ἔχῃ μένειν τὸ ὅρκιον κατὰ χώρην
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Herodotus states that one night when he was besieging Barca, Amasis the Persian general dug a wide trench and laid frail planks across it, which he then covered over with a layer of earth.
When day came, Amasis invited the Barcaeans to meet with him, and they agreed to a peace treaty. This was done while standing on the hidden trench. The two parties gave and accepted a sworn assurance that their treaty would hold good while the ground where they stood was unchanged.
When the sworn agreement was made, the Barcaeans opened their gates and came out of the city.
After this the Persians broke down the hidden bridge and ran into the city.
Herodotus states that they broke down the bridge in order that that they might keep the oath which they had sworn to the Barcaeans: namely, that this treaty would hold good for as long as the ground remained as it was; but if they broke the bridge the treaty no longer held good.
