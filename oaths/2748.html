Oath ID 2748: Lycurgus, Against Leocrates, 125, (literary, Orat., 330)


  Date: 410/409 (410)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
All Athenians (male, any adult age, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
All Athenians (male, any adult age, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Demophantus
 
  If taken: They can be citizens
  If refused: They cannot be citizens
  If kept: Many blessings (?)
  If broken: Destruction of self and family
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features Unblemished sacrifices (?)
 
  Statement: [That they would kill, by word and deed and vote and their own hand, if they could, anyone who overthrows the democracy at Athens and anyone who holds any office in the future after democracy has been overthrown, and anyone who sets himself up to be tyrant or helps set up a tyrant. And if anyone else kills the man, they would consider him pure in the eyes of both gods and spirits, because he has killed an enemy of the Athenians, and they would sell all the property of the dead man and give half to the killer, and would not hold any back. And if anyone dies in killing or attempting to kill any such man, they would care for both him and his children, just as for Harmodius and Aristogeiton and their descendants. And they declare null and void all oaths that have been sworn at Athens or in the camp or anywhere else against the people of Athens]
 
  Linguistic: wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Lycurgus refers here to the so-called oath of Demophantus. He claims that the Athenians swore that if anyone found a person aspiring to tyranny, or attempting to betray the city, or overthrew the democracy, he would be guiltless if he killed him (ἐάν τις τυραννίδι ἐπιτιθῆται ἢ τὴν πόλιν προδιδῷ ἢ τὸν δῆμον καταλύῃ, τὸν αἰσθανόμενον καθαρὸν εἶναι ἀποκτείναντα). He states that the oath was sworn after the overthrow of the Thirty, although the decree cited by Andocides states that the oath itself was taken after the overthrow of the Four Hundred in 410/09.
See also oath id 2358.
