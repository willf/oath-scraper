Oath ID 1601: Ergias, FGrH # 513, FGrH # 513, (literary, Myth., unknown)


  Date: mythical time

  State: Rhodes   Location: unknown

  Statement: [That Phalanthus and his men could take out of the city anything that they had in the belly and that Iphicles would provide them with boats]
Ὡς δὲ καὶ τοὺς ἰχθῦς εἶδεν, ὑπέλαβε
τὴν χώραν οὐκ ἔτι αὐτῶν εἶναι, καὶ ἐπεκηρυκεύσατο
πρὸς τὸν Ἴφικλον, ὑπόσπονδος ὑπεξελθεῖν ἀξιῶν μετὰ
τῶν σὺν αὑτῷ. Συγκαταθεμένου δὲ τοῦ Ἰφίκλου, ἐπι-

ὤμοσεν, ἐάσειν ἐξάγεσθαι ὅ τι κατὰ γαστρὸς αἴρωνται,
ἀντισοφίζεται, πλοῖα αὐτοῖς διδοὺς, ἵνα ἀποκομισθῶσι,

ὀμόσαι φήσας πλοῖα παρέξειν, ἄλλο δὲ οὐδέν.
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork, spond, wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Both Phalanthus and Iphicles technically keep their oaths, but each manages to bend the meaning of his oath. Phalanthus and his men try to smuggle gold and silver out of the city in the bellies of their animals, while Iphicles provides boats but no oars, sails or rudders. In fact, Iphicles had tricked Phalanthus into surrendering the city. There was an oracle that the city would hold until ravens became white and fish appeared in mixing bowls but Iphicles got a reliable servant to put fish into the mixing bowls and paint ravens with gypsum.
