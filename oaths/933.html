Oath ID 933: Aristophanes, Peace, 107-109, (literary, Comic., 421)


  Date: 421
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Outside Trygaeus' house
 
  Swearer:
Second Slave (male, mature, chattel-slave, n/a)
  Swearee:
Trygaeus (male, mature, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Emphasises the Slave's horror at his master's suggestion
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: [That Trygaeus will never indict Zeus for betraying Greece to the Medes as long as the Second Slave lives]
Γράψομαι
Μήδοισιν αὐτὸν προδιδόναι τὴν Ἑλλάδα.
{ΟΙ. Βʹ} Μὰ τὸν Διόνυσον οὐδέποτε ζῶντος γ’ ἐμοῦ—
 
  Linguistic: ma ton
 
  God(s) invoked:
Dionysus    
 
  Remarks: The statement "betraying Greece to the Medes" (as Sommerstein notes ad 107) refers to the fact that if Greeks continue to fight each other, they will not be able to fend off any new attack from the Persians (or Medes). The slave here is concerned for his master's safety, since challenging the gods in such a way is very dangerous for mortals (see further Sommerstein ad 109).
