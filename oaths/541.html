Oath ID 541: Homer, Odyssey, 12.298-307, (literary, Epic., date uncertain)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Thrinacia   Location: Off the coast
 
  Swearer:
Odysseus' men (male, mature, free, Ithacan)
  Swearee:
Odysseus (male, mature, power-holder, Ithacan)
 
  Proposed by:
Odysseus
 
  If taken: Odysseus will allow his men to beach on the island
  If refused: The men will not be allowed to beach on Thrinacia
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: no
  Impact: The men land on Thrinacia
  Consequences of breach: Death by drowning
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they will not slaughter any sheep or oxen they come upon on the island]
ἀλλ’ ἄγε νῦν μοι πάντες ὀμόσσατε καρτερὸν ὅρκον·
εἴ κέ τιν’ ἠὲ βοῶν ἀγέλην ἢ πῶϋ μέγ’ οἰῶν
εὕρωμεν, μή πού τις ἀτασθαλίῃσι κακῇσιν
ἢ βοῦν ἠέ τι μῆλον ἀποκτάνῃ· ἀλλὰ ἕκηλοι
ἐσθίετε βρώμην, τὴν ἀθανάτη πόρε Κίρκη.’
ὣς ἐφάμην, οἱ δ’ αὐτίκ’ ἀπώμνυον ὡς ἐκέλευον.
αὐτὰρ ἐπεί ῥ’ ὄμοσάν τε τελεύτησάν τε τὸν ὅρκον,
στήσαμεν ἐν λιμένι γλαφυρῷ εὐεργέα νῆα
ἄγχ’ ὕδατος γλυκεροῖο καὶ ἐξαπέβησαν ἑταῖροι
νηός, ἔπειτα δὲ δόρπον ἐπισταμένως τετύκοντο.
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork, wmnu
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Eurylochus urges the men to break this oath at 12.340-351 because they have exhausted their food supply. They follow his suggestions and break it at 12.353-365. The punishment for these actions is brought about by Zeus, after a threat from Helios. Zeus causes a shipwreck in which all the men drown, except Odysseus, who is washed up on Ogygie. This is all related in Odysseus' narrative to Alcinous.
