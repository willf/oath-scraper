Oath ID 1085: Aristophanes, Wasps, 478-479, (literary, Comic., 422)

  Reference type: Oath

  Statement: [That he'd be better off renouncing his father altogether rather than battling with all these troubles day after day]
νὴ Δί’ ἦ μοι κρεῖττον ἐκστῆναι τὸ παράπαν τοῦ πατρὸς
μᾶλλον ἢ κακοῖς τοσούτοις ναυμαχεῖν ὁσημέραι.
 
  Linguistic: nh
 
  God(s) invoked:
Zeus    
 
  Remarks: Like Bdelycleon's statement at id 1071, this is surely an exaggeration based on his frustrations at his present situation. As Sommerstein remarks ad 478: "in theory a son could [renounce his father] by having himself adopted by another (childless) man, who then took over all paternal rights in relation to him. For an only son to take this step, however, would certainly incur strong social disapproval as a dereliction of the son's duty to maintain his father in old age; and we know of no case in which an only son of a living father allowed himself to be adopted.... Yet Bdelycleon is so exasperated by the trials of his long struggle to cure Philocleon of his addiction to jury service that he wonders if this shocking action might not be the lesser evil".
