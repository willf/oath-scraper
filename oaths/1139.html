  Remarks: The text indicates that an action must be brought for the debt within a year (sc. or else not at all). The judge is to give judgement according to the testimony. The persons required to testify (sc. on behalf of the deceased) are his heirs, except that in the case of a judgement debt the judge and the mnemon must testify too. If they do testify, then "[the judge] shall judge, having sworn, himself and the witnesses, that they win the simple amount"; in other words, the witnesses must swear that the money in dispute is owed to (or is not owed by) the deceased's estate, and then the judge, having sworn the usual judicial oath, will decide in their favour. Nothing is said about oaths on the other side, but presumably they must have had an opportunity to swear too; however, the witnesses for the estate are evidently to be treated as horkioteroi.
