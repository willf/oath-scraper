Oath ID 3628: Demosthenes, Against Olympiodorus, 25, (literary, Orat., 343/2)


  Date: 343
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Callistratus (male, mature, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Pythodorus (the archon) (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: The case can be postponed
  If refused: The case cannot be postponed
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: see remarks
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That Olympiodorus was unable to attend due to military service]
ὑπωμοσάμεθα ἡμεῖς τουτονὶ Ὀλυμπιόδωρον δημοσίᾳ ἀπεῖναι στρατευόμενον.
 
  Linguistic: wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Callistratus tells the jurors that when the Archon summoned into court all those who were filing claims according to the law, he entered a sworn statement (the hypomosia), asking for a postponement on the ground that Olympiodorus was abroad on military service. However, their opponents lodged a counter-hypomosia (see oath id 3736) and convinced the jurors that Olympiodorus had left Athens on account of the trial.
