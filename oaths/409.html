  Remarks: This oath is volunteered by Hera in repsonse to a threat of violence by Zeus. Cf. oath ids 1334, 1358 and 1363 for divinities swearing by Zeus' head; cf. also oath id 160. Demosthenes Against Boeotus quotes part of the description here where the oath is said to be "the greatest and most awful" power among all mankind. He is highlighting the severity of swearing a false oath (in this case by Plangon) by quoting the Iliadic phrase which calls the water of the Styx "the greatest and most awful oath among the blessed gods". See oath id 3577. Hera is saying here that it is not by her will that Poseidon is harming the Trojans and helping the Achaeans; rather, she supposes [!] he must have taken it into his own head to do so. In fact, Poseidon would not have taken any action had he not been told by Sleep that it was safe to do so (14.354-360); and though Sleep was not acting on Hera's explicit orders, he knew very well that this, or something like it, was what Hera wanted – she was not going to all the trouble of putting Zeus to sleep, and making (and swearing to- see oath id 350) an extravagant promise to a minor god, just for fun – so that what Poseidon did was the predictable, intended and desired result of what Hera did and said. All the same, there was enough of a break in the chain of causation to keep the oath formally true.
