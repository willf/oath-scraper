Oath ID 833: Aristophanes, Ecclesiazusae, 934-935, (literary, Comic., Probably 391 (see Sommerstein))


  Date: Probably 391 (see Sommerstein) (391 to 390)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Outside Praxagora's house
 
  Swearer:
First Old Woman (female, old, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Girl (female, adolescent, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Irritates the Girl
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: [That the young man requires (or wants- see remarks) something from her]
οὐ σοῦ γ’, ὦλεθρε,
δεόμενος οὐδέν. {Γρ. α} νὴ Δί’, ὦ φθίνυλλα σύ
 
  Linguistic: nh
 
  God(s) invoked:
Zeus    
 
  Remarks: According to the new rules, the young man must first "screw" an old or ugly woman before he can "screw" his young girlfriend. δεόμενος could be interpreted as meaning either "requires" or "wants". It is true to say that Epigenes, if he is to obey both the law and his own desires, will need to sleep with the old woman; and it is not too big a stretch, either, to say that he will want to – but only in the sense in which one “wants” to, say, pay a parking fee (as the unavoidable but unwelcome cost of being allowed to go into, or in some cases out of, the car park) and may even be impatient to be allowed to pay it.
