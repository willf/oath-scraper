Oath ID 1338: Alcaeus, Fragments, 129.13-24, (literary, Lyr., 610-560)


  Date: Before Myrsilus came to power (612 to 608)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Mytilene   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Pittacus (male, mature, free, Lesbian)
Alcaeus (male, adolescent/mature, free, Lesbian)
Alcaeus' companions (male, mature, free, Lesbian)
  Swearee:
Pittacus (male, mature, free, Lesbian)

  Impact: Allegiance between the men formalised

  Remarks: Alcaeus here refers to Pittacus (son of Hyrrhas) "trampling the oaths of his fellow Lesbians" in joining the tyrant Myrsilus and sharing power with him. Myrsilus had come to power after the overthrow of Melanchrus, which we are told happened in the 42nd Olympiad (612-608). Alcaeus calls for an avenging curse to pursue Pittacus, but in fact, Pittacus would be chosen as dictator in 590 when Myrsilus died, and ruled till 580. For the trampling of oaths, cf. oath ids 1381 and 1512.
