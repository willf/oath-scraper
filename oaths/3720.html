  Remarks: Epichares claims that when Theocrines once launched a graphe paranomon against Demosthenes, and the latter sent someone to swear that he was unable to attend the trial due to illness (see oath id 3719), Theocrines did not swear a counter-hypomosia even though Demosthenes was going around abusing Aeschines.
The exact mechanics of this procedure are unclear. If a hypomosia is submitted on behalf of one party, justice might seem obviously to demand that the presiding magistrate should then turn to the other party and ask him "Have you got anyone who is willing to swear the contrary?" If both parties swore, the jury had to vote, after hearing speeches, on the question which of them was telling the truth (Dem. 48.25), and according to how they voted, either a postponement was allowed, or the case was decided against the absent party by default (see also oath id 3628 and 3736).
