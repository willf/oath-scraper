  Statement: [That he would not have enough money to pay his travelling expenses on the way home unless he should sell his horse and what he had about his person]
ἦ μὴν ἔσεσθαι μηδὲ ἐφόδιον ἱκανὸν οἴκαδε ἀπιόντι, εἰ μὴ ἀπόδοιτο τὸν ἵππον καὶ ἃ ἀμφ’ αὑτὸν εἶχεν.
 
  Linguistic: omos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Cleagoras who painted the murals at the Lyceum and Eucleides the Phliasian seer met Xenophon at Lampsacus.
Eucleides congratulated Xenophon on his safe return, and asked him how much gold he had got. When Xenophon swore that he would have to sell his horse and his possessions in order to bear the cost of returning home, Eucleides did not believe him.
When the Lampsacenes sent gifts of hospitality to Xenophon and he was sacrificing to Apollo, he gave Eucleides a place beside him. When Eucleides saw the vitals of the victims he said that he believed that Xenophon had no money.
