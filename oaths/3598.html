Oath ID 3598: Ephorus, Histories, Fr. 214 Jacoby, (literary, Hist., 360-330)


  Date: 370 to 369
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Thessaly   Location: Pherae
 
  Swearer:
Seven conspirators (male, adolescent, free, Thessalian?)
  Swearee:
Seven conspirators (male, adolescent, free, Thessalian?)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: They would be allowed to join the conspiracy
  If refused: They would not be allowed to join the conspiracy
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Conspiracy is kept secret
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would be loyal to each other and maintain the secrecy of their conspiracy]
 
  Linguistic: omos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: This is a reference to the assassination of Jason of Pherae, who had been chosen as ruler of Thessaly and was allegedly governing his subjects with moderation. Ephorus says he was murdered by seven young men who conspired together for the repute it would bring (cf. Xen. Hell. 6.4.31-2). Diodorus, in whom the fragment is preserved, says that other historians said that he was assassinated by his brother Polydorus (15.60.5). The two accounts are, of course, not incompatible, if e.g. it was suspected that the seven young men were in cahoots with Polydorus.
