Oath ID 3043: Isaeus, On the estate of Dicaeogenes, 7-8, (literary, Orat., ca.389)


  Date: 411
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
The husbands of the sisters of Dicaeogenes (II) (male, mature, free, Athenian)
Dicaeogenes (III) (male, mature, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
The husbands of the sisters of Dicaeogenes (II) (male, mature, free, Athenian)
Dicaeogenes (III) (male, mature, free, Athenian)

unknown

  Remarks: According to Menexenus, when Dicaeogenes (II) died, his uncle Proxenus (the father of Dicaeogenes III) produced a will which stipulated that Dicaeogenes (III) was to be recognised as the adopted son of Dicaeogenes (II), and heir to one third of his estate. The rest of the estate was to be divided equally among his sisters i.e. Menexenus' mother and her sisters.
Menexenus states that "our fathers" i.e. the husbands of the sisters of Dicaeogenes (II) agreed to this, and that when they had divided up the inheritance, they swore not to transgress the agreed terms, and each remained in possession of his share for twelve years.
At this time Dicaeogenes (III) brought a suit claiming that he had been adopted as the sole heir of Dicaeogenes (II), and thus deprived the sisters of Dicaeogenes (II) of their inheritance.
Dicaeogenes II died in a sea-battle off Cnidus, probably that of Syme in early 411 (Thuc. 8.42), and the division of the inheritance remained in effect for twelve years, i.e. from 412/1 until 401/0. Dicaeogenes III therefore advanced his claim in 401/0; the reference to stasis does not require us to assume an earlier date, since 401/0 was the year in which the democrats reconquered the mini-state of Eleusis which had been granted to the oligarchs in 403. The date of the oath, therefore, is 411.
