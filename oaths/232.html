Oath ID 232: IG I(3), 76, 17-21, (inscriptional, Public, ca.422 or 417/6)


  Date: ca.422 or 417/6 (427 to 416)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Bouleutai (male, mature, free, Bottiaean)
Generals (male, mature, power-holder, Bottiaean)
Other Archai (male, mature, power-holder, Bottiaean)
  Swearee:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: Their alliance would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be an alliance
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would be friends and allies to the Athenians faithfully and guilelessly; would consider their friends and enemies to be the same as those of the Athenians; would not aid the enemies of the Athenians in any way; and would not bear malice on account of the past.]
φίλοι ἐσόμε[θα Ἀθεναίοις καὶ χσύμ]μαχοι πιστο̃[ς] κα[ὶ] ἀδόλος καὶ τ[ὸς αὐ]τὸ[ς φίλος καὶ ἐχθ]ρὸς νομιο̃με[ν] hόσπερ ἂν Ἀθενα[ῖοι], καὶ ο[ὐκ ὀφελέσο τὸ]ς ἐχθρὸς τὸς Ἀθεναίον οὔτε χρ[έμα]σ̣ιν h[απλο̃ς οὔτε δυ]νάμει οὐδεμιᾶι, οὐδὲ μνεσικ[ακέσο] το̣̃ν [παροιχομέν]ον ἕνεκα·
 
  Linguistic: omnu, summac
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: This inscription records an alliance between Athens and poleis in the Bottiae.
This entry takes into account textual changes suggested in Agora 16.16.
Five Athenians were elected to receive the oath of the Bottiaeans. The names of three Bottiaean poleis are extant: Calindoea, Tripoae and Cemacae.
For the corresponding oath of that Athenians see oath id 231.
