Oath ID 3824: Demosthenes, On the False Embassy, 204, (literary, Orat., 343)

  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following terms of the peace:
1. That the other Greeks who were not parties to the Peace of Philocrates shall be free and autonomous;
2. That if anyone marches against any of them, those who are parties to the peace will go to the aid of the threatened state]
 
  Linguistic: eirhn, wmot

  Remarks: Demosthenes argues that Aeschines cannot say that it is advantageous that the Phocians have been destroyed, and Philip holds Thermopylae, and the Thebans are in power, and there are soldiers in Euboea, and there's a plot against Megara, and the peace is unsworn.
This cannot be a reference to the Peace of Philocrates. Cawkwell, CQ 13, (1963), pp133-4, argues that Demosthenes is referring to an amendment to the Peace of Philocrates proposed in 344/3, which would have guaranteed freedom and autonomy to all Greek states. This would effectively turn the Peace of Philocrates into a Common Peace, but with Philip and Athens (rather than Persia) as its guarantors.
