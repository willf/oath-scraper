Oath ID 1821: Thucydides, History, 8.75.2-3, (literary, Hist., 403-395)


  Date: 411
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Samos   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Samians (male, any adult age, free, Samian)
Athenian stratiotai (male, adolescent/mature, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Thrasybulus (male, mature, power-holder, Athenian)
Thrasyllus (male, mature, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Thrasybulus
 
  If taken: They will be safe
  If refused: There will be a threat of force
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would in truth maintain a democracy and live in harmony, and prosecute zealously the war against the Peloponnesians, and be enemies of the Four Hundred, and not make overtures to them]
ἦ μὴν δημοκρατήσεσθαί τε καὶ ὁμονοήσειν καὶ τὸν πρὸς Πελοποννησίους πόλεμον προθύμως διοίσειν καὶ τοῖς τετρακοσίοις πολέμιοί τε ἔσεσθαι καὶ οὐδὲν ἐπικηρυκεύσεσθαι
 
  Linguistic: ork, wmnu, wrk
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Thucydides states that when the Athenians at Samos had learned that the Four Hundred had seized control, Thrasybulus and Thrasyllus (being openly in favour of changing the government at Samos to a democracy) bound all the Athenian soldiers and the Samians of military age by the most solemn oaths that they would maintain a democracy, and live in harmony, continue to fight against the Peloponnesians, and be enemies of the Four Hundred.
Thucydides states that they focused in particular on "the oligarchs".
After this the Athenians deposed the generals and trierarchs they suspected of being oligarchs.
