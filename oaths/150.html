Oath ID 150: IG I(3), 40, 21-39, (inscriptional, Public, 446/5 or 424/3)


  Date: 446/5 or 424/3 (446 to 423)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Chalcis   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Those (men) who have attained puberty (male, any adult age, free, Chalcidian)
Envoys (male, mature/old, free, Chalcidian)
  Swearee:
Envoys (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
Oath-commissioners (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Diognetus
 
  If taken: unknown
  If refused: Atimia, property confiscated
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would not rebel against the Athenian demos by word or deed, in any way whatever, either by their own volition or by the persuasion of another. If anyone did rebel they would denounce him to the Athenians. They would pay the tribute to the Athenians which they would persuade them to assess, and as an ally they would be the best and truest possible, and they would assist the Athenian demos and defend them if anyone injures the Athenian demos, and they would obey the Athenian demos]
οὐκ ἀπο[σ]τέσομαι ἀπὸ το̃ [δ]έμο το̃ Ἀθεναίον οὔτε τέ[χ]νει οὔτε μεχανε̃ι οὐδεμιᾶι οὐδ’ ἔπει οὐδὲ ἔργοι οὐδὲ το̃ι ἀφισταμένοι πείσομαι, καὶ ἐὰν ἀφιστε̃ι τις κατερο̃ Ἀθεναίοισι, καὶ τὸν φόρον hυποτελο̃ Ἀθεναίοισι, hὸν ἂν πείθο Ἀθεναίος, καὶ χσύμμαχος ἔσομαι hοῖος ἂν δύνομαι ἄριστος καὶ δικαιότατος καὶ το̃ι δέμοι το̃ι Ἀθεναίον βοεθέσο καὶ ἀμυνο̃, ἐὰν τις ἀδικε̃ι τὸν δε̃μον τὸν Ἀθεναίον, καὶ πείσομαι το̃ι δέμοι το̃ι Ἀθεναίον.
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork, summac
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: This inscription records the regulations imposed on Chalcis by Athens.
The tithe consecrated from the confiscated property was to go to Zeus Olympios.
The swearing of the oath at Chalcis was to be overseen by Athenian presbeis and the "horkotai". Lines 45ff refer to the election of five Athenians as horkotai.
The oath is described in lines 40ff as being exchanged just as it was with the Eretrians (see oath id 146 and 147).
For the corresponding oath of the Athenians see oath id 148. The decree and oaths were to be inscribed on marble stelai at the expense of the Chalcidians. The Athenian copy was placed on the acropolis, and the Chalcidian copy in the temple of Zeus Eleutherios.
The decree ends with the word hόρκος as if the text of the oath would be inscribed there, but it was not done so, presumably because it has already been incorporated into the body of the decree.
See also oath id 3861.
