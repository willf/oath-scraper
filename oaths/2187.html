Oath ID 2187: Xenophon, Anabasis, 4.8.7, (literary, Hist., ca.370-355)


  Date: 401
  Reference type: Oath
  State: The land of the Macronians   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Macronian)
Greek generals (male, mature, power-holder, various)
  Swearee:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Macronian)
Greek generals (male, mature, power-holder, various)
 
  Proposed by:
Macronians
 
  If taken: The treaty would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a treaty
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: the Macronians helped the Greeks cut down trees to build a bridge
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features The exchange of spears
 
  Statement: [That they would not harm each other]
ὅτι οὐ κακῶς γε ποιήσοντες
 
  Linguistic: martu
 
  God(s) invoked:
The gods    
 
  Remarks: When the Greeks were met by a force of Macronians in battle array they asked who they were.
The Macronians responded by identifying themselves, and asking why the Greeks were coming against their land.
The Greeks responded that they had not come to do them any harm whatsover, and that they had been at war with the King and were on their way back to Greece.
The Macronians asked whether they would give pledges to this effect and the Greeks replied that they were ready to give and receive pledges.
Thereupon the Macronians gave the Greeks a barbarian spear and the Greeks gave them a Greek spear, for the Macronians declared that these were pledges. Both sides called upon the gods to witness.
