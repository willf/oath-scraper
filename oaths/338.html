Oath ID 338: Rhodes and Osborne, GHI, 88, 23-46, (inscriptional, Public, mid-fourth century)


  Date: 479
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Plataea   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Athenian hoplites (male, adolescent/mature, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Athenian hoplites (male, adolescent/mature, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: unknown
  If refused: unknown
  If kept: May their polis be free from sickness, and remain unsacked; may the land bear fruit; may women bear children like to their parents; may cattle bear young like to cattle
  If broken: May their polis be afflicted with sickness and be sacked; may the land be barren; may the women and the cattle bear monsters
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features Blood sacrifices (covered with shields)
 
  Statement: [That they would fight while they lived, and not put life before being free, and not desert the taxiarch nor the enomotarch, neither while they (the officers) live nor when they are dead, and that they would not depart unless the leaders led the way, and they would do whatever the generals command, and they would bury in the same place the dead of those who were allied and leave none unburied, and when they had been victorious fighting against the barbarians they would (totally destroy and) dedicate a tenth of the city of the Thebans, and that they would not raze Athens or Sparta or Plataea or any of the other poleis that were allied, nor let them be coerced by hunger, nor keep them from running water, whether they were friends or enemies]
μαχοῦμαι ἕως ἂν ζῶ, καὶ οὐ περὶ πλέονος ποήσομαι τὸ ζῆν ἢ τὸ ἐλεύθερος εἶναι, καὶ οὐκ ἀπολείψω τὸν ταξίαρχον οὐδὲ τὸν ἐνωμοτάρχην οὔτε ζῶντα οὔτε ἀποθανόντα, καὶ οὐκ ἄπειμι ἐὰμ μὴ οἱ ἡγεμόνες ἀφηγῶνται, καὶ ποήσω ὅ τι ἂν οἱ στρατηγοὶ παραγγείλωσιν, καὶ τοὺς ἀποθανόντας τῶν συμμαχεσαμένων θάψω ἐν τῶι αὐτῶι καὶ ἄθαπτον οὐδένα καταλείψω· καὶ νικήσας μαχόμενος τοὺς βαρβάρους δεκατεύσω τὴν Θηβαίων πόλιν, καὶ οὐκ ἀναστήσω Ἀθήνας οὐδὲ Σπάρτην οὐδὲ Πλαταιὰς οὐδὲ τῶν ἄλλων πόλεων τῶν συμμαχεσαμένων οὐδεμίαν, οὐδὲ τῶν λιμῶι περιόψομαι ἐργομένους οὐδὲ ὑδάτων ναματιαίων εἴρξω οὔτε φίλους ὄντας οὔτε πολεμίους. καὶ εἰ μὲν ἐμπεδορκοίην τὰ ἐν τῶι ὅρκωι γεγραμμένα, ἡ πόλις ἡμὴ ἄνοσος εἴη, εἰ δὲ μή, νοσοίη· καὶ πόλις ἡμὴ ἀπόρθητος εἴη, εἰ δὲ μή, πορθοῖτο· καὶ φέροι ἡμή, εἰ δὲ μή, ἄφορος εἴη· καὶ γυναῖκες τίκτοιεν ἐοικότα γονεῦσιν, εἰ δὲ μή, τέρατα· καὶ βοσκήματα τίκτοι ἐοικότα βοσκήμασι, εἰ δὲ μή, τέρατα.
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: This text was set up in the mid-fourth century, and records the oath sworn prior to the battle of Plataea.
Herodotus does not mention this oath, although he speaks of a similar oath being sworn at Thermopylae.
Theopompus (fr. 153) dismissed the oath as ficticious (see oath id 3649), but Lycurgus quoted an almost identical oath (oath id 2747) and so did Diodorus (11.29.2f) presumably when using Ephorus. Both of these sources place the taking of the oath at the Isthmus.
For a discussion which argues that this oath is an altered version of the oath of the sworn bands at Sparta (oath id 1272), see Hans van Wees, "The Oath of the Sworn Bands, the Acharnae stele, the Oath of Plataea and archaic Spartan warfare", in A. Luther. M. Meier, & L. Thommen (eds.), Das Fruhe Sparta, Franz Steiner, Stuttgart 2006, pp.125-64.
This stone also records the ancestral oath sworn by the Athenian ephebes (see oath id 340).
