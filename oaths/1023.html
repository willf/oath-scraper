Oath ID 1023: Aristophanes, Knights, 832-835, (literary, Comic., 424)


  Date: 424
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Outside Demos' house
 
  Swearer:
Sausage-seller (male, adolescent, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Paphlagon (male, mature, chattel-slave, n/a)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: May he die
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: Emphasises his determination
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: [That he'll prove that Paphlagon took a bribe from Mytilene of more than forty minas]
Καί σ’ ἐπιδείξω
νὴ τὴν Δήμητρ’, ἢ μὴ ζῴην,
δωροδοκήσαντ’ ἐκ Μυτιλήνης
πλεῖν ἢ μνᾶς τετταράκοντα.
 
  Linguistic: mh zwihn, nh thn
 
  God(s) invoked:
Demeter    
 
  Remarks: The charge of taking a bribe from Mytilene is, as Sommerstein remarks ad 834, "precisely the charge that Cleon, in the Assembly debate of 427 on the fate of that city after its unsuccessful rebellion, had himself made against his opponent Diodotus (Thuc. 3. 38. 2)." Here "a charge originally made by Cleon against another is nonsensically turned against Cleon himself". Forty minas is not a large bribe, but Sommerstein suggests ad 835 that "the gravamen of the charge lies not so much in the amount of the bribe as in Cleon's alleged failure to fulfil his side of the alleged bargain; for in two successive debates he had advocated the execution or enslavement of the entire Mytilenean people (Thuc. 3.36-40) and in the end over a thousand men were put to death under a decree proposed by him (Thuc. 3.50.1).
