Oath ID 760: IG XII 5, 2, 4-7, (inscriptional, Public, fourth century)


  Date: Fourth century (399 to 300)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Ios   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Those possessing flocks (male, any adult age, free, Iot)
  Swearee:
Hieopoioi (male, mature/old, free, Iot)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: They will not have to pay a fine
  If refused: They shall pay a fine of 100 drachmas to the demos and 50 to whoever denounces him
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: n/a   True:
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they do not hold any flocks which they have not declared]
ὁμόσα[ι δὲ καὶ ἄλλ]ο μὴ νεμεν
 
  Linguistic: omos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The text indicates that the hieropoioi are to announce to those possessing flocks that they are to burn sacrifices for all the flocks they hold, and if they do not hold flocks, to swear that they do not hold any flocks which they have not declared (this means one must interpret νέμεν as a present rather than a future infinitive).
Whoever does not swear within an uncertain number of days will owe 100 drachmas to the demos and up to half of that sum to whoever denounces him.
We may reasonably suppose that there was an official list of flock-holders somewhere; that anyone who swore that he was not (or no longer) a flock-holder was removed from the list; and that the list was policed by unfriendly neighbours who could be relied on to inform the authorities if anyone not on the list was illicitly keeping sheep.
