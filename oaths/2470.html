Oath ID 2470: Antiphon, On the murder of Herodes, 11-12, (literary, Orat., 420-413)


  Date: 420 to 413
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Prosecutor (male, any adult age, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Presiding magistrate (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Presiding magistrate
 
  If taken: He can prosecute for murder
  If refused: The case cannot proceed
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: Destruction of self, kin and house
 
  Taken: no   True:
  Impact: n/a
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features Holding sacrificial vicitms
 
  Statement: [That he knew well that the defendant had killed]
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Euxitheus, the defendant, argues that he ought to be being tried in a homicide court rather than a Heliastic court, and that the prosecutor should have sworn the preliminary oath (διωμοσία). He describes the diomosia as "the most solemn and binding oath known", and argues that the prosecutor should have taken this oath "under pain of causing yourself and your kin and your house to perish, that you would accuse me only in connexion with the murder itself, to the effect that I committed it".
It is striking that Eux. says only that in a dike phonou his prosecutor would have had to swear with special solemnity that he would speak strictly to the point - not that he’d have had to swear with special solemnity that Eux. was guilty. Presumably he doesn't want to risk reminding the jury that in the preliminary proceedings to this trial the prosecutor had sworn (though not with special solemnity) that Eux. was guilty!
