Oath ID 1515: Thucydides, History, 1.9.1, (literary, Hist., 403-395)


  Date: Mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: Tyndareus' palace
 
  Swearer:
Helen's suitors (male, adolescent/mature, power-holder, various)
  Swearee:
Tyndareus (male, old, power-holder, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: unknown
  If refused: unknown
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: The expedition against Troy
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would support whoever became Helen's husband should someone take her from the house, and that they would all march against him and sack his city with force of arms, be he Greek or Asiatic]
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Thucydides argues that the expedition to Troy came about because of the power of Agamemnon compared to the princes of his time, rather than because the suitors were bound by their oaths to Tyndareus.
For other references to this oath see Euripides, Iphigenia Aulidensis 58-65, 78, 391 and 395 (oath id 182-185) and Sophocles, Philoctetes 72 (oath id 280). See also Isocrates 10.40.
