  Remarks: Apparently the allies were convinced of the need for the alliance by the Assyrian king's suggestions that the Medes and the Persians, who had intermarried, and were powerful nations, would be likely to make war on each of the other nations and subjugate them. Those who were unconvinced by such arguments were apparently bribed to join the alliance. Cf. oath ids 2326, 2385 and 2389. The allies subsequently begin to desert- see oath id 2387. In book 6, the Indian spies report to Cyrus that Croesus has been made leader and general over all the allied kings. They also report that Thracian swordsmen had been hired and 120,000 armed Egyptians were sailing to join them (see oath id 2449). Indians were listed amongst the original allies (see oath id references above) but are not mentioned here for obvious reasons, and the Carians are also absent, though Lycaonians, Arabians, Phoenicians, Ionians, Aeolians and almost all the Greek colonists in Asia have now seemingly joined the alliance. Also, in book 6, the Assyrian king, whose father had originally proposed the alliance has fled and left his troops under the king of Babylon. The Hyrcanians, subjects of Assyria, had previously deserted- see oath id 2387. Now, Cyrus reminds his troops how Croesus fled rather than standing by his allies, when he saw the Syrians (i.e. the Assyrians) defeated (cf. oath id 2387).
