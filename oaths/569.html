  Remarks: The statement is unknown. Although this decree is very fragmentary it is clear that four lines (2, 4-6) refer to oaths. Line 4 indicates that an official would receive oaths, line 5 seems to be what remains of the statement of a penalty for violating an oath, and line 6 seems to indicate that the oath shall be binding if someone desires or is willing to do something. These four lines are as follows: <o>
2) [ὑ]πὸ τοῖν hόρϞια
4) δέξεται τὸς hόρϞος
5) αἴτ’ hόρϞον ὅτι ἁ ὠφέλε[ια - - - αἴτ’ hόρϞον ὅτι]
6) - - hόρϞος ἐνδέτο, αἰ λο̃(ο)ι τὸν το - - - </o>
