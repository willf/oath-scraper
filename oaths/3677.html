Oath ID 3677: Demosthenes, Against Conon, 40-42, (literary, Orat., 355 or 341)


  Date: 355 or 341 (355 to 341)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Dikasterion
 
  Swearer:
Ariston (male, adolescent, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Dikastai (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Conon
 
  If taken: His statement will carry more weight
  If refused: He will not be believed
  If kept: many blessings
  If broken: Destruction of self
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That he did suffer suffer at Conon's hands the insults of which he accuses him, that he was dealt those blows, and that his lip was split so badly that it needed to be stitched, and that he is suing him because he has been the victim of hybris]
ὀμνύω τοὺς θεοὺς καὶ τὰς θεὰς ἅπαντας καὶ πάσας ὑμῶν ἕνεκ’, ὦ ἄνδρες δικασταί, καὶ τῶν περιεστηκότων, ἦ μὴν παθὼν ὑπὸ Κόνωνος ταῦθ’ ὧν δικάζομαι, καὶ λαβὼν πληγάς, καὶ τὸ χεῖλος διακοπεὶς οὕτως ὥστε καὶ ῥαφῆναι, καὶ ὑβρισθεὶς τὴν δίκην διώκειν.
 
  Linguistic: oloi, omnu, omos, ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
All the gods and goddesses    
 
  Remarks: Ariston has his challenge (proklesis) read to the jurors, and states that he was willing to swear to it then, and that he is willing to do so in court.
After swearing he stresses that he is not perjuring himself, "not even if Conon explodes with indignation".
He goes on to argue that he has not only explained his claims, but added a (sworn) pledge to them.
(NB he isn’t actually bringing a charge of hybris, only of aikeia, but he claims throughout the speech that Conon could, on the facts, have been validly charged with hybris.)
