  Remarks: According to Xenophon, in 395 the leading men in Thebes were aware that unless someone began the war the Spartans would not break the treaty with their allies. They resolved to stir up trouble between Sparta's allies, which would then force the Spartans to intervene.
Accordingly, they persuaded the Opuntian Locrians to levy money from territory which was in dispute between Phocis and Locris, for they thought that if this was done Phocis would invade Locris. This did happen, whereupon Androcleides persuaded his fellow Thebans to make a counter invasion of Phocis on the grounds that the Locrians were their friends and allies. This alliance must have come about when the Thebans resumed their alliance with the Spartans in 421/0. Both the Thebans and the Locrians were members of the Peloponnesian League and were thus allied to each other.
When Phocis appealed to Sparta for assistance, war between Sparta and Thebes ensued.
