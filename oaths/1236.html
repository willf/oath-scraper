  Remarks: The First Athenian refers to a potential oath here sworn falsely during the drunken meeting in the Acropolis (off-stage). It is impossible to know whether such an oath was actually sworn, or whether this is just a "Sophoclean" usage (where a character treats an unsworn statement as an oath- see oath id 236 with cross references). The sentence also seems to be cut short by the return of the slaves (see Sommerstein ad 1238). Telamon was a warlike song whereas Cleitagora began with a reference to “peace and prosperity”, so that a less generous-minded Athenian (like the ambassadors referred to in 1231-5) might have seen the choice of song as evidence that the Spartans were really as hawkish as ever. For a similar situation, cf. Xen. Cyr. I.iii.10.12 in oath id 2313.
