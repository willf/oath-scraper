Oath ID 1741: Thucydides, History, 5.59.4-61.2, (literary, Hist., 403-395)


  Date: 418
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Agis (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
Thrasyllus (male, mature/old, power-holder, Argive)
Alciphron (male, mature/old, free, Argive)
  Swearee:
Agis (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
Thrasyllus (male, mature/old, power-holder, Argive)
Alciphron (male, mature/old, free, Argive)
 
  Proposed by:
Thrasyllus
 
  If taken: The truce would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a truce
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would abide by the truce for four months, during which time they would settle their dispute by arbitration]
 
  Linguistic: eirhn, speis, spend, spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: King Agis made a truce with the Argives for four months, within which time they would fulfil their agreement i.e. to settle their dispute by arbitration and make a treaty for peace.
Agis was persuaded by Thrasyllus, one of the five generals, and Alciphron the Argive proxenos for Sparta. Thucydides states that these men were acting on their own initiative rather than at the behest of the Argive people.
Thucydides observes that the allies were angry with Agis and the Argives were angry with those who had made the truce without consulting them. They tried to stone Thrasyllus to death, but he escaped to a nearby sanctuary.
However, when an Athenian infantry and cavalry force arrived the Argives told them to go away, because they were afraid to break the truce with the Spartans.
The Athenians protested that it was not right to have made a truce without the consent of the rest of the allies.
For other references to this truce and its impact see oath id 1743.
