  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following terms:
1. All states should withdraw their governors from other cities;
2. They should all disband their armies and fleets;
3. All poleis should be autonomous;

  Remarks: Xenophon contrasts the behaviour of the Athenians and the Spartans after the truce was formalised.
Whereas the Athenians withdrew their garrisons and recalled the forces led by Iphicrates (see oath id 1975), the Spartans withdrew their harmosts and garrisons, but did not send out orders to King Cleombrotus who was at the head of the army in Phocis. Cleombrotus asked the authorities at home for instructions, and the matter was debated at Sparta.
Prothous addressed the assembly, saying that it seemed to him that they ought to disband the army in accordance with their oaths, and then send round word to the cities to make contributions to a war fund. Then, if anyone tried to prevent the cities from being autonomous, they should call together all who wished to support the cause of independence and lead them against those who opposed it.
Prothous argued that this would ensure that the gods would be most favourably inclined towards them, and the cities least annoyed.
The Spartan assembly, however, decided that he was talking nonsense, and sent orders to Cleombrotus not to disband his army, but to lead it against the Thebans if they did not leave the cities autonomous.
When Cleombrotus learned that the Thebans had not disbanded their army but were planning to lead it against him, he led his troops into Boeotia.
For other references to the Common Peace of 371 see oath id 1984.
