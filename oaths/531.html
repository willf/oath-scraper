Oath ID 531: IG II(2), 1183, 8-13, (inscriptional, Public, third quarter of the 4th century)


  Date: Third quarter of the 4th century (349 to 325)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Hagnous or Myrrhinous
 
  Swearer:
Scrutineer (euthynos) (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Demarch (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Demarch
 
  If taken: He can act as a scrutineer (euthynos)
  If refused: He cannot act as scrutineer
  If kept: Many good things
  If broken: the opposite
 
  Taken: n/a   True:
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would avoid acting unjustly, not themselves, nor through anyone else on their behalf, nor with their knowledge, or by any other manner or means. And if anyone seemed to be in the wrong they would condemn him at the scrutiny and punish him as the offence seemed to them to deserve]
[- - - - οὔτ’ αὐτὸς ἐγὼ] οὔτ[ε] ἄ[λ]λος ἐμοὶ οὐ[δὲ ἄ]λλ̣ε̣(ι) εἰδότος ἐ̣μο[ῦ μηχανῆι ἢ τ]έ[χνη]ι οὐδεμιᾶι· κ̣α̣ὶ ἐὰν μ[ο]ι [δ]οκεῖ ἀδικεῖν κα[τευθ]υν[ῶ] α[ὐτ]οῦ [καὶ τιμήσ]ω οὗ [ἂ]ν μ[ο]ι [δ]οκεῖ ἄξιον εἶναι τὸ ἀδί[κ]ημα· [νὴ τ]ὸ[ν] Δ[ία ν]ὴ τὸ[ν] Ἀπόλλω νὴ τὴ[ν Δ]ήμ<η>τρα, εὐορ<κ>οῦντι μ[έν] <μ>οι πολ[λ]ὰ κα[ὶ ἀγα]θά, εἰ δ’ ἐπιορκο[ί]ην τἀναντία·
 
  Linguistic: nh thn, nh ton, omnu, ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
Zeus    
Apollo    
Demeter    
 
  Remarks: The beginning of the oath of the scrutineers is lost.
This entry follows the text in Rhodes and Osborne, GHI, 63. Kirchner identified the deme as Myrrhinous. Rhodes and Osborne suggest Hagnous.
For the oath of the reckoner see oath id 535. For the oath of the advocates see oath id 536. See also oath id 538.
The text indicates that the demarch will oversee the ballot for those participating in the scrutiny and will exact their oaths.
