Oath ID 3577: Demosthenes, Against Boeotus 2, 10, (literary, Orat., ca.347)


  Date: 369 to 364
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Plangon (female, mature, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Mantias (male, mature, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Plangon
 
  If taken: Her statement will carry more weight
  If refused: She will not be believed
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: no
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: see remarks
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That if she were paid thirty minas, she would get her brothers to adopt Boeotus and Pamphilus, and that if Mantias challenged her before the arbitrator to swear that the children were in very truth his sons, she would decline the challenge]
ὡμολόγησεν τριάκοντα μνᾶς λαβοῦσα τούτους μὲν τοῖς αὑτῆς ἀδελφοῖς εἰσποιήσειν υἱεῖς, αὐτὴ δέ, ἂν πρὸς τῷ διαιτητῇ προκαλῆται αὐτὴν ὁ πατήρ μου ὀμόσαι ἦ μὴν τοὺς παῖδας ἐξ αὑτοῦ γεγονέναι, οὐ δέξεσθαι τὴν πρόκλησιν·
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Mantitheus claims that Plangon deceived his father "by an oath that among all mankind is held to be the greatest and most awful". Mantitheus is highlighting the severity of swearing a false oath by quoting the Iliadic phrase which calls the water of the Styx "the greatest and most awful oath among the blessed gods" (see oath id 409).
Mantitheus states that his father agreed to the plan, because he believed that if Plangon declined to swear that Boeotus and Pamphilus were his sons, they would no longer be able to cause him problems.
What is this "greatest and most formidable" oath - in the human world, of course, not the divine? We are given no details in either of the speeches (here and in 39.3). Are we meant to infer that Plangon called down destruction on herself and her offspring? Perhaps - but if so, it is fairly certain that we are meant to infer a falsehood, since if Plangon had broken an oath taken in such terms, Mantitheus, suing her son, could not have failed to mention it explicitly.
For other references to this oath see oath id 3561.
