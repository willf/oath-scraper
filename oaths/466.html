Oath ID 466: IG II(2), 204, 7-10, (inscriptional, Public, 352/1)


  Date: 352/1 (352 to 351)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Eleusinion (in the city)
 
  Swearer:
Arcephron of Lamptrae (bouleutes 1) (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
[---] of Thria (bouleutes 2) (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
[---] of Hagnous (bouleutes 3) (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
Bouleutes #4 (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
Bouleutes #5 (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
Idiotes #1 (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
Hippocrates of Cerameis (idiotes 2) (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)

[---] of Sunium (idiotes 6) (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
Aristides of Oe (idiotes 7) (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)

Glaucon of Perithoedae (idiotes 9) (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)

  Swearee:
Presiding magistrate (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Presiding magistrate
 
  If taken: They would be eligible to decide on the new boundary (and place the new boundary markers)
  If refused: They would be ineligible to decide on the new boundary (and place the new boundary markers)
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would not vote according to favour or hostility, but as uprightly and piously as they could]
ὀ[μόσαντας τὸν νόμιμον ὅρκον ἦ μὴν] μήτε χάριτος ἕνεκα μὴτ’ ἔ[χθρας ψηφιεῖσθαι, ἀλλὰ ὡς δι]καιότατα καὶ εὐσεβέστατα·
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The text indicates that 15 Athenians (five bouleutai and 10 men from all the Athenians) were elected to decide in the Eleusinion in the city (not at Eleusis) about the dispute between Athens and Megara over the boundaries of the sacred land.
It is stated that they would swear "the customary oath".
