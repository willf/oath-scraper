  Remarks: Xenophon writes, "were it apparent that you were striving to make the shrine at Delphi autonomous as it used to be, not by joining in war, but by sending embassies up and down Greece, I for my part should not be surprised if you found all the Greeks of one mind, bound together by oaths and allies against anyone who attempted to seize the shrine in the event of the Phocians abandoning it".
The key clause is "in the event of the Phocians abandoning it". In any realistic world, they were never going to be induced to abandon it except (i) by force or (ii) by a guarantee against collective punishment (which might just have persuaded them to disown, and deliver up, the leaders responsible for seizing the shrine). In event (i) it would certainly be Thebes, not Athens, that would earn the prestige. If Xenophon is thinking clearly (which may be doubted), he must be thinking in terms of event (ii), and suggesting that Athens propose the foundation of a new Hellenic League (perhaps a strengthened version of the Amphictyonic League) with its members bound by a clear treaty commitment to keep the peace with each other and to use force against any future violator of Delphi (or, presumably, against anyone who attacked a member of the League).
