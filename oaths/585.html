  Remarks: The editor of IvO identified the script as West-Greek and the dialect as Doric. Roehl tentatively suggested that it might have come from Laconia, but that was rejected by the editor of IvO.
However, the ending -essi, while typically Aeolic, is also found in some north-western dialects, alongside -ois [sic] - and Elean is among them. And, crucially, the appearance of ζὲ for δὲ is characteristic of early Elean and with one exception is apparently not found in any truly Doric dialect of the Peloponnese. It follows that the dialect of this inscription is Elean (no later than 550).
This very fragmentary text contains two references to the taking of oaths. In frag a, line 5, there is a reference to "the oath ceremonies" (τὰ hό[ρκια]), and in frag b, line 5 the text has been restored to read [τ]οῖς εὐhορκεό[ντεσσι], and line 6 has been restored to read τῶι ζὲ hόρ[κωι], or τοῖζε hορ[ίοις]. Exactly what the benefits for keeping these oaths were cannot be determined with the evidence we have.
