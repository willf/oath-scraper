Oath ID 116: Euripides, Medea, 390-398, (literary, Trag., 413)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Corinth   Location: Outside Jason and Medea's house
 
  Swearer:
Medea (female, mature, free, Colchian)
  Swearee:
Chorus (female) (n/a, mature, free, Corinthian)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Highlights Medea's resolve
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: [That Medea will not let any of her enemies rejoice at vexing her heart (because, one way or another, she will kill them)]
ἢν μέν τις ἡμῖν πύργος ἀσφαλὴς φανῆι,
δόλωι μέτειμι τόνδε καὶ σιγῆι φόνον·
ἢ δ’ ἐξελαύνηι ξυμφορά μ’ ἀμήχανος,
αὐτὴ ξίφος λαβοῦσα, κεἰ μέλλω θανεῖν,
κτενῶ σφε, τόλμης δ’ εἶμι πρὸς τὸ καρτερόν.
οὐ γὰρ μὰ τὴν δέσποιναν ἣν ἐγὼ σέβω
μάλιστα πάντων καὶ ξυνεργὸν εἱλόμην,
῾Εκάτην, μυχοῖς ναίουσαν ἑστίας ἐμῆς,
χαίρων τις αὐτῶν τοὐμὸν ἀλγυνεῖ κέαρ.
 
  Linguistic: ma thn
 
  God(s) invoked:
Hecate Epithet(s) dwelling in the innermost part of the hearth
 
  Remarks: Mastronarde (2002) ad 397 remarks: 'Hecate was worshipped outdoors, in streets, and at crossroads, so Medea's statement that the goddess dwells within her house, virtually in displacement of Hestia, conveys both an exotic transgression of the norm and a special personal intimacy'.
