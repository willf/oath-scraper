  Statement: [That Spurge has never yet desired to eat so much, nor Tree-trunk to wear a cloak, nor has Nile ever desired barley-groats so much, nor has Lark ever desired so much to eat for free]
μὰ τοὺς θεούς,

  Remarks: Spurge, Treetrunk, Nile and Lark are all nicknames. The speaker is apparently in love (line 1), and he (it must be he) is saying that the four persons he names have never desired their respective greatest pleasures as much as he desires … whoever she is. Spurge is a glutton, Treetrunk and Nile are both very poor, and Lark is a parasite (mentioned frequently in other comedy and elsewhere). We do not know the real names of any of the four; three of the nicknames are fairly obviously related to physical peculiarities (Lark to shape of head) and “Nile” to a real or alleged Egyptian connection of some sort.
