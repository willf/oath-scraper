Oath ID 1778: Aristotle, Athenaion Politeia, 23.5.2-4, (literary, Phil., 335-322)


  Date: 478
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Delos   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Aristides (male, mature, power-holder, Athenian)
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
  Swearee:
Aristides (male, mature, power-holder, Athenian)
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
 
  Proposed by:
Aristides
 
  If taken: Their alliance(s) would be formalised
  If refused: There would be no alliance(s)
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: Delian League formed
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features Lumps of iron were allowed to sink to the bottom of the sea
 
  Statement: [That they would have the same enemies and friends, and they would remain loyal and not desert (the Athenian masses or the allies of the Athenians)]
καὶ τοὺς ὅρκους ὤμοσεν τοῖς Ἴωσιν ὥστε τὸν αὐτὸν ἐχθρὸν εἶναι καὶ φίλον
 
  Linguistic: ork, wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The author states that Aristides administered oaths to the Ionians two years after the battle of Salamis, in the archonship of Timosthenes (478/7). Their oaths were ratified by letting lumps of iron sink to the bottom of the sea.
The author goes on to state that the Athenians would later treat their allies too masterfully, except Chios, Lesbos and Samos.
The exact statement of the oath for the so-called Delian League is unknown. The author of the Ath.Pol. indicates here that they swore oaths "to have the same enemies and friends". Herodotus indicates that after Mycale the Greeks swore "to remain loyal and not desert". IG I(3)15 indicates that the Erythraeans later swore to "not desert the Athenian masses nor the allies of the Athenians".
Plutarch, Aristides 25, states that Aristides took the oath on behalf of the Athenians, and later urged the Athenians to put the perjury of breaking their oaths on his head, and to use that to their advantage.
