Oath ID 1977: Xenophon, Hellenica, 6.4.12, (literary, Hist., 385-354)


  Date: 411-362 i.e. the time that Xenophon is describing (411 to 362)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
A Spartan youth (male, adolescent, free, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Other members of the sworn band (male, any adult age, free, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: He could be part of a sworn band
  If refused: He could not
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach:
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That he would fight while he was alive, and would not regard being alive as more important than being free; that he would not leave his taxiarch or his enomotarch, whether he was alive or dead, and that he would bury the dead among his fellow-fighters, and that he would leave no-one unburied]
 
  Linguistic: wmot
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Xenophon observes that at the battle of Leuctra in 371, King Cleombrotus led the three enomotiai (i.e. the "sworn bands" that each Spartan joined as an adolescent) abreast, so that the phalanx was not more than twelve men deep.
See also oath id 1272.
