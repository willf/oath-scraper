Oath ID 99: Euripides, Cyclops, 262-269, (literary, Satyr., ?408)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sicily   Location: Foot of Mt. Etna
 
  Swearer:
Silenus (male, old, chattel-slave, n/a)
  Swearee:
Cyclops (male, mature, free, n/a)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: May Silenus' wicked sons, whom he loves dearly, perish miserably
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: Provokes counter-oath by satyrs
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: [That Silenus is not selling the Cyclops' property to the strangers]
μὰ τὸν Ποσειδῶ τὸν τεκόντα σ’, ὦ Κύκλωψ,
μὰ τὸν μέγαν Τρίτωνα καὶ τὸν Νηρέα,
μὰ τὴν Καλυψὼ τάς τε Νηρέως κόρας,
μὰ θαἰερὰ κύματ’ ἰχθύων τε πᾶν γένος,
ἀπώμοσ’, ὦ κάλλιστον ὦ Κυκλώπιον,
ὦ δεσποτίσκε, μὴ τὰ σ’ ἐξοδᾶν ἐγὼ
ξένοισι χρήματ’. ἢ κακῶς οὗτοι κακοὶ
οἱ παῖδες ἀπόλοινθ’, οὓς μάλιστ’ ἐγὼ φιλῶ.
 
  Linguistic: ma thn, ma ton, wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
Poseidon Epithet(s) Cyclops' father
Triton Epithet(s) Great
Nereus    
Calypso    
Nereids    
Sacred waves    
All species of fish    
 
  Remarks: Silenus' oath is probably to be treated as true. The present infinitive could be equivalent either to a present or to an imperfect tense (since both derive from the present stem). If it stands for an imperfect, Silenus is lying. If it stands for a present, however, he is telling the truth, since he is not now engaged in selling anything to the newcomers. See also oath ids 98 an 101. Cf. oath id 1484 for the invocation of Poseidon in the context of the Cyclops.
