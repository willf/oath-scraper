Oath ID 479: IG II(2), 236, frags a and b, (inscriptional, Public, 338/7)


  Date: 338/7 (338 to 337)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Corinth   Location: unknown

  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown

  Statement: [That they would abide by the peace (?) and neither break the agreement with Philip (?) nor take up arms for harm against any of those who abide by the oaths (?) neither by land nor by sea; nor would they take any polis or guardpost nor harbour, for war, from any of those participating in the peace, by any craft or contrivance; nor would they overthrow the kingdom of Philip or his descendants, nor the constitutions existing in each state when they swore the oaths concerning the peace; nor would they do anything contrary to these agreements, nor allow anyone else as far as possible; if anyone did commit any breach of treaty concerning the agreements, they would go in support as called on by those who are wronged (?) and would make war against the one who transgresses the common peace (?) as decided by the common synedrion and the hegemon; and they would not abandon...]

  Remarks: The text of the "League of Corinth" treaty followed here is Rhodes and Osborne, GHI, 76. The text of this inscription is very fragmentary and subject to debate.
There are two fragments:
Fragment A includes the actual oath;
Fragment B is a (fragmentary) list of the poleis included in the peace.
