Oath ID 1956: Xenophon, Hellenica, 5.1.30-5, (literary, Hist., 385-354)


  Date: 387/6 (387)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Agesilaus (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
Envoys (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
  Swearee:
Agesilaus (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
Envoys (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
 
  Proposed by:
Artaxerxes II
 
  If taken: Peace treaty formalised
  If refused: Artaxerxes would wage war on them by land and by sea, with ships and with money
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would abide by the peace which the King sent down]
αἱ πόλεις ἐμμενεῖν ἐν τῇ εἰρήνῃ ἣν κατέπεμψε βασιλεύς
Artaxerxes imposed his terms in the following letter:
"King Artaxerxes thinks it just that the cities in Asia should belong to him, as well as Clazomenae and Cyprus among the islands, and that the other Greek cities, both small and great, should be left independent, except Lemnos, Imbros, and Scyros; and these should belong, as of old, to the Athenians. But whichever of the two parties does not accept this peace, upon them I will make war, in company with those who desire this arrangement, both by land and by sea, with ships and with money".
 
  Linguistic: omnu, ork, spond, wmnu, wmwm
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Xenophon states that the envoys from all the Greek poleis swore that they would observe the provision laid down by the King.
However, the Theban envoys claimed the right to swear on behalf of all the Boeotians.
Agesilaus refused to accept the oaths unless they swore just as the King's letter directed: that every city whether small or great should be independent.
When the Theban envoys refused, Agesilaus told them to go home and report to the people that they would be shut out of the treaty.
Agesilaus then prepared a force to lead against Thebes and marched out to Tegea. But before he left Tegea the Theban envoys returned with word that the Thebans would leave the other Boeotian cities independent. Agesilaus then returned home and the Thebans were forced to accede to the treaty.
The Argives also withdrew from Corinth and the Corinthians regained their independence.
Xenophon states that when these things had been accomplished and the states had sworn that they would abide by the treaty the armies and navies were disbanded.
