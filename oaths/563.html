Oath ID 563: Homer, Odyssey, 24.482-486, (literary, Epic., date uncertain)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Ithaca   Location: Odysseus' palace
 
  Swearer:
Odysseus (male, mature, power-holder, Ithacan)
Suitors' families (male, mature/old, free, Achaean)
  Swearee:
Odysseus (male, mature, power-holder, Ithacan)
Suitors' families (male, mature/old, free, Achaean)
 
  Proposed by:
Zeus
 
  If taken: Odysseus and the suitors will be at peace
  If refused: The conflict will continue
  If kept: They will have prosperity and peace in abundance
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Peace in Ithaca and the restoration of Odysseus to the kingship
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That Odysseus will be accepted as king “for ever” (which probably implies accepting Telemachus as his heir) and will not be pursued over the deaths of the suitors, and that they will be friends to each other as formerly]
ἐπεὶ δὴ μνηστῆρας ἐτείσατο δῖος Ὀδυσσεύς,
ὅρκια πιστὰ ταμόντες ὁ μὲν βασιλευέτω αἰεί,
ἡμεῖς δ’ αὖ παίδων τε κασιγνήτων τε φόνοιο
ἔκλησιν θέωμεν· τοὶ δ’ ἀλλήλους φιλεόντων
ὡς τὸ πάρος, πλοῦτος δὲ καὶ εἰρήνη ἅλις ἔστω.”
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The oath is proposed here by Zeus, and it is reported as sworn (with Athene, in the guise of Mentor, administering it) at oath id 564. The phrase "cut trustworthy oaths" is unique here in the Odyssey but is common in the Iliad (cf. e.g. oath ids 316, 317, 318) because it is used in oaths between factions.
