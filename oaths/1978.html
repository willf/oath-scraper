Oath ID 1978: Sophocles, Antigone, 758-759, (literary, Trag., just before the Samian expedition 441-440)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Thebes   Location: Palace of Creon
 
  Swearer:
Creon (male, mature, power-holder, Theban)
  Swearee:
Haemon (male, adolescent, free, Theban)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Creon's punishments lead Haemon to take his own life
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: [That Haemon will not continue to insult him with impunity]
ἀλλ’ οὐ, τόνδ’ Ὄλυμπον, ἴσθ’ ὅτι,
χαίρων ἔτι ψόγοισι δεννάσεις ἐμέ.
 
  Linguistic: ou + acc.
 
  God(s) invoked:
Olympos    
 
  Remarks: The informal oath formula οὐ + acc., current in Doric and Arcadian (cf. oath ids 575, 576, 1217, 1219, 1230, 2214, 2307), was not used in Attic, and in tragedy it is found only in Sophocles (see oath ids 244, 1979, 1980) and mostly in lyric (this is the only instance in spoken iambics). The linguistic abnormality may be designed to mark the extremity of Creon's anger; his next words are to order that Antigone be brought out of the palace and put to death before her fiancé's eyes, an order which is not carried out only because Haemon immediately storms off, declaring (erroneously) that his father will never set eyes on him again.
