Oath ID 2883: Plato, Phaedo, 89b-c, (literary, Phil., 384-379)


  Date: 550 to 546
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Argos   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Argives (male, any adult age, free, Argive)
  Swearee:
World at large (n/a, n/a, unknown, n/a)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: They would prove their determination to recover Thyrea
  If refused: They would not prove their determination to recover Thyrea
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: Argives are subject to a self-imposed curse
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That no Argive should grow his hair long, and none of their women should wear gold jewellery, until they recovered Thyrea]
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The reference is to an event mentioned by Herodotus 1.82.7. The Argives had lost Thyrea to the Spartans as a result of the "Battle of the Champions" and "from that time cut their hair, whereas they had previously been required to grow it long, and made a law and a curse that no Argive should grow his hair long, and none of their women should wear gold jewellery, until they recovered Thyrea". Hdt. places these events in the time of Croesus, apparently not long before the fall of Sardis; c. 550-546. The Argives never did recover Thyrea, but they did try to get Thyrea (also called Cynuria) back by negotiation in 421 and 420 as the price of renewing their peace treaty with Sparta, but the Spartans wouldn't hear of it (Thuc. 5.14.4, 5.41.2). They invaded the territory in 414 and took much booty (Thuc. 6.95.1), with which they made a major dedication at Delphi (Paus. 10.9.12); perhaps they reckoned then that on this occasion they'd held the area long enough to discharge their vow. Cf. oath id 3599 where the Spartans manage to keep their oath but still send men back to Sparta after having sworn an oath not to return home again until they had either destroyed Messene or were all killed.
