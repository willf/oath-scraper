Oath ID 3045: Isaeus, On the estate of Dicaeogenes, 31, (literary, Orat., ca.389)


  Date: 405 to 390
  Reference type: Oath

  Remarks: In sections 16-30, Menexenus (III) indicates that Menexenus (II) - the cousin of Menexenus (III) - took Dicaeogenes to court and had the will showing him as sole heir declared invalid. Leochares was subsequently successfully prosecuted for bearing false witness.
Menexenus agreed to ignore the potential penalty provided that Dicaeogenes (III) agreed to give up two-thirds of the estate of Dicaeogenes (II) to the sisters of Dicaeogenes (II), and Leochares agreed to act as surety. Mnesiptolemus of Plotheia also agreed to act as surety.
However, according to Menexenus, both Dicaeogenes (III) and Leochares soon denied that the agreement had been made.
Thus, Menexenus commenced proceedings against Leochares for failing to guarantee the handing over of the property by Dicaeogenes (III).
When the action against Leochares was coming on, both Leochares and Dicaeogenes (III) met with Menexenus and his cousins and asked them to postpone the action and submit the matter to arbitration. They agreed, and after four arbitrators were chosen they swore to abide by their decision (see oath id 3046, 3047).
See also oath id 3048.
