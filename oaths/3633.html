Oath ID 3633: Demosthenes, Against Olympiodorus, 51, (literary, Orat., 343/2)


  Date: 343
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Callistratus (male, mature, free, Athenian)
Olympiodorus (male, mature, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Callistratus (male, mature, free, Athenian)
Olympiodorus (male, mature, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: Their agreement would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be an agreement
  If kept: unknown

  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: see remarks
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would in truth divide fairly and honestly all the visible property of Comon, and that neither would in any respect take advantage of the other in regard to Comon's estate, that they would make joint inquiry for the rest, and would act in common in forming whatever plans should from time to time be necessary]
 
  Linguistic: ork, wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
The gods    
 
  Remarks: Callistratus tells the jurors that Androcleides is present with the articles of the sworn agreement. He says that he gives consent for them to be opened during the defendant's speeches, and tells the jurors that he wishes for them to hear the agreement and the oaths that he and Olympiodorus swore to each other (see oath id 3623). He argues that if Olympiodorus refuses to read the oaths and agreements to the jurors it will be clear that he is the most shameless of men, and the jurors will be thus correct to refuse to accept anything that he says as true.
There’s something a trifle fishy about this whole story, if we apply the regular principle that if two people confirm a business deal by oaths that means that they don’t fully trust each other; and it’s all the more remarkable in view of the fact that they were brothers-in-law.
