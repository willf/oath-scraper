Oath ID 1746: Thucydides, History, 5.76.3-80.1, (literary, Hist., 403-395)


  Date: 418/7 (418)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Argos   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Argive)
Representatives (male, any adult age, power-holder, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Argive)
Representatives (male, any adult age, power-holder, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
Spartans
 
  If taken: Peace treaty formalised
  If refused: Hostilities will continue
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: The Spartans returned home
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following terms:
1. The Argives would restore to the Orchomenians their children, to the Maenalians their men and to the Spartans the men they had installed at Mantinea
2. The Argives were to evacuate Epidaurus and demolish the fortifications they had built there
3. If the Athenians did not withdraw from Epidaurus they were to be enemies of both Sparta and Argos (and the allies of both)
4. The Spartans were to restore any children to their homelands
5. Concerning the offering to Apollo Pythaeus (which the Epidaurians had been accused of not making cf. Thucydides 5.53.1), the Argives could impose an oath upon the Epidaurians, or alternatively could swear it themselves (see oath id 1747 and 1760)
6. If anyone from outside the Peloponnese should invade the Peloponnese they shuld repel the invader, taking counsel with the other Peloponnesians
7. They should convey this agreement to their other allies and make terms with them as seemed best, but if the allies preferred they were to be allowed to send the treaty home for consideration.]
 
  Linguistic: spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Thucydides states that the Spartans marched out to Tegea and then sent envoys to Argos regarding a peace treaty. According to Thucydides a faction opposed to democracy convinced the Argives to make peace, after which the Spartans returned home.
