Oath ID 2098: Xenophon, Anabasis, 2.3.27-9, (literary, Hist., ca.370-355)


  Date: 401
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Persia   Location: Assyria
 
  Swearer:
Tissaphernes (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
The brother of the King's wife (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
  Swearee:
Greek generals (male, mature, power-holder, various)
Greek lochagoi (male, mature, power-holder, various)
 
  Proposed by:
Tissaphernes
 
  If taken: The treaty would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a treaty
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: no
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features Clasping of right hands
 
  Statement: [That the territory the Greeks pass through shall be friendly, and that they shall lead the Ten Thousand back to Greece without treachery, providing them with a market; and wherever they do not furnish the Ten Thousand with a market they will allow them to take provisions from the country]
ὑμῖν τὴν χώραν καὶ ἀδόλως ἀπάξειν εἰς τὴν Ἑλλάδα ἀγορὰν παρέχοντας· ὅπου δ’ ἂν μὴ ᾖ πρίασθαι, λαμβάνειν ὑμᾶς ἐκ τῆς χώρας ἐάσομεν τὰ ἐπιτήδεια.
 
  Linguistic: wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Three days after Tissaphernes spoke with the Greeks he returned with a message from the King that they would be spared.
Tissaphernes and the brother of the King's wife swore that they would lead the Greeks back home and provide them with supplies.
The Greeks on their part were to swear that they would proceed as they would through a friendly country and take no food from the land unless the Persians did not provide them with a market (see oath id 2105).
After the oaths were exchanged Tissaphernes returned to the King, having said that he would return fully equipped to take the Greeks back home and return to his own satrapy.
For other references to this oath see oath id 2109, 2114, 2171, 2173, 2174, 2176, 2178, 2179, 2181, 2184.
