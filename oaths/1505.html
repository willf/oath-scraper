Oath ID 1505: Herodotus, The Histories, 9.26, (literary, Hist., 428-415)


  Date: Mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Peloponnese   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Peloponnesian kings (male, any adult age, power-holder, various)
Hyllus (male, mature, power-holder, none)
  Swearee:
Peloponnesian kings (male, any adult age, power-holder, various)
Hyllus (male, mature, power-holder, none)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: Their agreement would be formalised
  If refused: There would be no agreement
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Echemus, King of Tegea, slew Hyllus in battle
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That if Hyllus should defeat the Peloponnesian champion, the Heraclidae should return to the land of the their fathers, but if he were defeated, the Heraclidae should go back and lead their army away and not seek to return for one hundred years]
ἢν μὲν Ὕλλος νικήσῃ τὸν Πελοποννησίων ἡγεμόνα, κατιέναι Ἡρακλείδας ἐπὶ τὰ πατρώια, ἢν δὲ νικηθῇ, τὰ ἔμπαλιν Ἡρακλείδας ἀπαλλάσσεσθαι καὶ ἀπάγειν τὴν στρατιὴν ἑκατόν τε ἐτέων μὴ ζητῆσαι κάτοδον ἐς Πελοπόννησον
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: According to Herodotus, when a dispute arose between the Tegeates and the Athenians regarding who should hold the opposite wing to that held by the Spartans, the Tegeates cited this precedent to justify their claim to hold the wing.
