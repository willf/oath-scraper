  Remarks: When Cyrus was sent to take control of Persian affairs in the west, he came upon Pharnabazus conducting Athenian envoys to the King. These envoys had been sent during the aborted siege of Calchedon, and wished either to be allowed to see the King or to return home.
Cyrus ordered Pharnabazus to hand them over or not to send them home yet, because he did not wish the Athenians to know what was going on.
Pharnabazus then detained them for three years (or three months) to ensure that Cyrus did not have cause to censure him, but later asked Cyrus to allow him to send them home, claiming that he had given them his oath to conduct them back to the coast if he could not conduct them to the King. (Perhaps significantly, there is no mention of this part of the oath sworn by Pharnabazus in 1.3.9-10: see oath id 1883 and 1885).
The envoys were then sent to Ariobarzanes who escorted them to Kios in Mysia, and from there they sailed to join the Athenian army.
