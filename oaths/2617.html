  Statement: [That they would not destroy any Amphictyonic polis or to bar access to flowing water in war or peace, and to take the field against anyone who broke his oath and destroy his city; and if anyone were to rob the god's property or connive in or advise any action against the contents of the temple, they would punish him with hand and foot and voice and all their power]
μηδεμίαν πόλιν τῶν Ἀμφικτυονίδων ἀνάστατον ποιήσειν, μηδ’ ὑδάτων ναματιαίων εἴρξειν μήτ’ ἐν πολέμῳ μήτ’ ἐν εἰρήνῃ, ἐὰν δέ τις ταῦτα παραβῇ, στρατεύσειν ἐπὶ τοῦτον καὶ τὰς πόλεις ἀναστήσειν, καὶ ἐάν τις ἢ συλᾷ τὰ τοῦ θεοῦ, ἢ συνειδῇ τι, ἢ βουλεύσῃ τι κατὰ τῶν ἱερῶν, τιμωρήσειν καὶ χειρὶ καὶ ποδὶ καὶ φωνῇ καὶ πάσῃ δυνάμει·
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
Apollo?    
 
  Remarks: Aeschines refers to the first synod of the Amphictyons, and states that he informed Philip about this oath, and told him that it seemed unjust to let the Boeotian cities lie in ruins.
He states that he listed the twelve peoples who shared responsibility for the temple (stressing that the Thebans were not the only Boeotians included), and showed that each - both great and small - had an equal vote.
