  Statement: [That not even if he'd got hare's milk and been eating peacocks (could he) gobble up so much money on his own]

  Remarks: The translation is based on Arnott's suggestion (ad loc.), with autos interpreted as “on his own”, i.e. without the assistance of an extravagant mistress. He further comments ad loc. that the fragment "could be spoken by an angry father (or paedagogus or friend) claiming that he himself would never have been so extravagant, or by the infatuated lover picking up his accuser's words and trying to refute them". He also notes (ad 3) that the oath μὰ τὴν γῆν (and μὰ γῆν) is used in comedy only by male speakers (where the sex is identifiable).
