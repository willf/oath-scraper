  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following terms:
1. Free access to the oracle at Delphi (the Spartans promised to try to persuade the Boeotians and Phocians about this)
2. Those who had misused the treasures at Delphi were to be sought out
3. Each side should retain what territory it currently possessed (the Athenian garrison at Pylos should keep within the coastal high points Buphras and Tomaeus; the Athenian garrison in Cythera should have no communication with the territory of the Spartan allies; the Athenian garrison in Nisaea and Minoa should not cross the roads leading to the mainland and the Megarians should not cross it either; the Athenians should retain the island [Atalante?] which they took and neither side shall communicate with the other; the Athenians should retain whatever territory they possessed in Troezen in accordance with the agreements which the Troezenians had made with the Athenians)
4. The Spartans and their allies were not permitted to sail in oared ships except along their own coasts, and then only in vessels of less than 500 metra capacity not designed for use in war
5. Deserters should not be received
6. Any disputes should be resolved by arbitration]
 
  Linguistic: spend, spond, wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Thucydides describes here the one year armistice between Athens and Sparta in 423.
The Athenian swearers were all generals. It seems likely that the Peloponnesians who swore the oath were also power-holders.
Thucydides also quotes the Athenian decree proposed by Laches which endorsed the armistice.
For other references to this oath and its impact, see oath id 1677, 1678 and 1680.
