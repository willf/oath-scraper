  Statement: [That at the time Timarchus was not yet as ugly as now, but still usable]
οὔπω μὰ Δία ὥσπερ νῦν ἀργαλέος ὢν τὴν ὄψιν, ἀλλ’ ἔτι χρήσιμος
 
  Linguistic: ma
 
  God(s) invoked:
Zeus    
 
  Remarks: Aeschines states that after Timarchus and his friends physically abused Pittalacus and he went as a suppliant to the altar of the Mother of the Gods in the agora, Timarchus went to Pittalacus and touched his chin in supplication, and said that he would comply with all of Pittalacus' wishes. Aeschines' oath is part of his explanation as to why Pittalacus was won over by Timarchus; by "usable" he means, of course, "tolerable as a sexual partner", and Timarchus' promise to do whatever Pittalacus wanted is meant to be interpreted in the light of that.
