Oath ID 2628: Aeschines, On the Embassy, 155-6, (literary, Orat., 343)


  Date: 346
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Dikasterion
 
  Swearer:
Aristophanes (male, mature/old, free, Olynthian)
Dercylus, son of Autocles, of Hagnous (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
Aristides, son of Euphiletus, of Cephisia (male, mature/old, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Presiding magistrate (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Presiding magistrate
 
  If taken: His evidence will carry more weight
  If refused: It will not
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: The exact statement is unknown.
 
  Linguistic: omnu
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Aeschines calls Aristophanes of Olynthus to testify that Demosthenes had tried to bribe him to give false evidence against Aeschines - namely that he had committed a drunken assault on one of Aristophanes' female relatives (see Demosthenes, On the False Embassy 196-8); in addition Dercylus and Aristeides testify that they had heard this story from Aristophanes and reported it to Aeschines.
After they give evidence, Aeschines says to the jurors, "You have heard the witnesses swearing solemnly and testifying".
Perhaps one of the witnesses (Aristophanes?) volunteered an oath (to make his testimony more impressive - or maybe merely because it was the normal practice at Olynthus where he came from?)
