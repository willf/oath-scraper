Oath ID 1325: Herodotus, The Histories, 3.144-147, (literary, Hist., 428-415)


  Date: post ca.522 (522 to 515)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Samos   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Maendrius (male, mature, power-holder, Samian)
  Swearee:
Otanes (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
 
  Proposed by:
Maendrius
 
  If taken: Their truce would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a truce
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: no
  Impact: The Persians entered the city believing that they were safe
  Consequences of breach: Otanes slew all the Samians who fought against him
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would not attack each other]
 
  Linguistic: speis, spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Herodotus states that when Otanes (one of the so-called 'Seven' who overthrew the Magi) led a Persian force to Samos to install Syloson the brother of Polycrates in power on Samos, Maendrius the tyrant and nephew of Syloson offered to leave the island under a truce (ὑπόσπονδοί). Otanes agreed to this, and upon the treaty being made (σπεισαμένου) the Persians entered the city.
After this, Charilaus the brother of Maendrius persuaded him to breach the agreement. Maenadrius left the city as agreed, but once he had done so Charilaus led a force against the Persians who were taken unaware, "believing a full agreement had been made" (δοκέοντάς τε δὴ πάντα συμβεβάναι).
After seeing the great harm done to the Persians, Otanes ignored the orders given to him by Darius to deliver the island unharmed to Syloson and commanded his soldiers to kill all the Samian men and boys they captured.
