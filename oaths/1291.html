  Remarks: Here, Hesiod explains how Zeus made Styx (the river) "the great oath of the gods", i.e. she became the deity to be invoked in oaths made by gods; cf. oath ids 350 and 409. By 'oath' (horkos) is here meant 'the divinity to be invoked when swearing'. The circumstances for taking this oath are defined, not by the content of what was being asserted, but by the identity of the swearer (a divinity) and the perceived need for special solemnity. West comments ad loc.: "Styx is to the gods much as Horkos is to men… A god takes an oath by Styx, not merely mentioning her name, but by making a libation of water… This constitutes an effective curse on the perjurer". Cf. also oath ids 1289 and 1295.
