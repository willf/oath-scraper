Oath ID 1097: IC IV, 72, Col II, ll 36-45, (inscriptional, Public, about 450)


  Date: About 450 (460 to 440)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Gortyn   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
The citizen who captured the defendant (male, any adult age, free, Gortynian)
Four others (male, any adult age, free, Gortynian)
  Swearee:
unknown (n/a, unknown, unknown, unknown)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: He will be believed
  If refused: He will not be believed
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: Solemn curses upon himself
 
  Taken: n/a   True:
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That he took him in adultery, and not by subterfuge]
μοικίοντ’ ἐλέν, δολόσθαι δὲ μέ
 
  Linguistic: omos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The text indicates that if anyone caught in the act of adultery declares that he has been taken by subterfuge, the captor is to swear an oath that he captured him in adultery and not by subterfuge. In cases involving fifty staters or more i.e. cases involving the seduction of a free woman the captor is to swear along with four witnesses.
For examples where men of different status swore this oath see oath id 1098 and 1099.
