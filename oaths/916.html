Oath ID 916: CID I, 9, Face A, ll 1-18; Face B, ll 26-34; Face C, ll 3-6, (inscriptional, Public, late fifth or early fourth century)


  Date: Late fifth or early fourth century (424 to 350)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Delphi   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Tagos (male, mature/old, power-holder, Delphian)
  Swearee:
Outgoing tagoi (male, mature/old, power-holder, Delphian)
 
  Proposed by:
Outgoing tagoi
 
  If taken: He can serve as tagos
  If refused: He cannot serve as tagos
  If kept: Many good things
  If broken: Evil instead of good
 
  Taken: n/a   True:
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: a fine of ten drachmas
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That he would serve as tagos justly, according to the laws of the polis and the Labyadai, regarding the offerings and sacrificial victims and of cakes; He would exact money and publish accounts justly for the Labyadai and would not steal nor do any harm by any means or device to the property of the Labyadai, and he would make the tagoi for the next year swear the oath according to as it is written: I promise by Zeus Patroios. If I keep my oath may good things (happen to me) and if I break my oath may evil (happen to me)- evil instead of good]
τα̣γ̣ε̣[υ]σέω δι[κ]α[ίως κ]α[τ]ὰ τοὺν ν̣όμους [τ]ᾶς [π]ό[λι]ος καὶ το[ὺ]ς τῶν Λαβυαδ[ᾶν] πὲρ τῶν ἀ[π]ελ[λ]αίων καὶ [τᾶ]ν δαρατᾶν̣· κ̣α̣ὶ̣ τ̣ὰ̣ χρήμ[α]τ[α] συμπραξέ[ω] κ[ἀ]ποδειξέω [δι]καίως τοῖς̣ Λα̣β̣υ̣άδαις [κ]ωὔτε κλεψέω οὔτε βλα[ψ]έ[ω ο]ὔ̣τε τέχνηι οὔτε μαχα̣νᾶ̣[ι] τ̣ῶν τῶλ Λαβυαδᾶ̣ν̣ χρημ̣[άτ]ων καὶ τὸς ταγοὺ[ς ἐπ]αξ̣έ̣ω̣ τὸν hόρκον τοὺς ἐ[ν ν]έω[τ]α κὰτ τὰ γεγρ̣αμμέν̣α̣. hόρκ̣ος· hυπίσχομα̣ι̣ ποὶ τοῦ Δ[ι]ὸς τοῦ Πατ̣ρώιου· εὐορκέον̣τι μέμ μοι ἀ̣γα̣θὰ̣ εἴη, αἰ [δ’] ἐ̣φιορκέοιμ[ι, κ]α̣κῶν (?) τ̣ὰ κ[α]κὰ ἀντὶ τῶν ἀγ[α]θῶν.
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork, wmot
 
  God(s) invoked:
Zeus    
 
  Remarks: The text also indicates that anyone who serves as tagos without swearing the oath is to pay a fine of fifty drachmas. For the oath sworn by a tagos who disputed being prosecuted for violating these rules see oath id 917.
