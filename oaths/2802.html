  Remarks: At this stage of the funeral oration Lysias urges the Athenians to remember the men who fought for the cause of democracy against King Pausanias of Sparta. He states that they had "as allies their oaths and their agreements".
Todd, Lysias, p38 n.21, argues that the term "'oaths and agreements' at 2.62 is a standard term for the amnesty of 403/2".
However, the events being commemorated occurred during, not after, the civil war; the men referred to all belonged to - indeed, constituted - the Piraeus party; and they are described as "having for enemiesâ€¦their own fellow-citizens". Todd himself, in another of his footnotes, says correctly that the amnesty came after the fighting here spoken of. The "oaths and agreements" spoken of must therefore be those taken by the democrats among themselves, resolving to fight together for the overthrow of the Thirty.
