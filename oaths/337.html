  Statement: [That it is a swallow (and therefore spring)]
νὲ τὸν hερακλέα
 
  Linguistic: nh ton
 
  God(s) invoked:
Heracles    
 
  Remarks: This vase-painting was first published by Guarducci, EG 3, pp467-8.
Three figures are watching a swallow. The figure on the left (a beardless youth) points to the swallow and says "Look, a swallow" (ἰδοῦ, χελιδόν). The figure in the middle (a bearded man) points to the swallow, saying, "By Heracles" (νὲ τὸν hερακλέα). The figure on the right (a young boy), with open hand states, "There it is" (hαυτεί). The painter himself writes, "It is spring already" (ἔαρ ἔδε).
