Oath ID 160: Euripides, Helena, 835-839, (literary, Trag., 412)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Egypt   Location: At Proteus' tomb
 
  Swearer:
Helen (female, mature, free, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Menelaus (male, mature, free, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: Menelaus is satisfied that no other man shall have his wife
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features hand clasp
 
  Statement: [That if their plan fails and Menelaus is killed, she will never accept another husband (i.e. Theoclymenus) but will kill herself with the same sword]
Ελ. ἀλλ’ ἁγνὸν ὅρκον σὸν κάρα κατώμοσα ...
Με. τί φήις; θανεῖσθαι; κοὔποτ’ ἀλλάξεις λέχη;
Ελ. ταὐτῶι ξίφει γε· κείσομαι δὲ σοῦ πέλας.
Με. ἐπὶ τοῖσδε τοίνυν δεξιᾶς ἐμῆς θίγε.
Ελ. ψαύω, θανόντος σοῦ τόδ’ ἐκλείψειν φάος.
 
  Linguistic: ork, wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
Menelaus' head    
 
  Remarks: As with the previous oath in this play (348), this oath is also conditional. It states what is to happen should Helen fail to persuade Theonoe to help them escape. In fact Theonoe will be persuaded and the fulfillment of the oath will not be required. See id 162 for Menelaus' reciprocal oath. It is noteworthy that Helen swears by Menelaus' head. Divinities swear by Zeus' head (cf. oath ids 409, 1334, 1358, 1363), but this is the only example of an oath by a mortal's head.
