Oath ID 3638: Demosthenes, Against Timotheus, 10, (literary, Orat., 362?)


  Date: 378/7 (378)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
Alcetas (male, mature/old, power-holder, Epirot)
Jason of Pherae (male, mature, power-holder, Thessalian)
Representatives of various poleis (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
  Swearee:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
Alcetas (male, mature/old, power-holder, Epirot)
Jason of Pherae (male, mature, power-holder, Thessalian)
Representatives of various poleis (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
 
  Proposed by:
Aristoteles
 
  If taken: The alliance(s) would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be any alliance(s)
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following terms:
1. All allies were to be free and autonomous, with no garrisons or tribute required;
2. The Athenians would give up any cleruchies, and would not be permitted to occupy and land belonging to the allies;
3. The parties in involved would defend each other if attacked]
 
  Linguistic: summac
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Apollodorus refers to Alcetas and Jason as allies of the Athenians, and says that they spoke on behalf of Timotheus at his trial in 373/2.
Alcetas and his son Neoptolemus are named as allies of Athens at lines 109-110 of IG II(2) 43. The name on the next line was deliberately erased in antiquity. The name of Jason has often been restored in this rasura. Cf. Rhodes and Osborne, Greek Historical Inscriptions 404-323 B.C., (Oxford 2003), p105.
For other references to the Second Athenian League see oath id 2974.
