Oath ID 2224: Xenophon, Lacedaemonian Politeia (except ch.14), 11.4, (literary, Hist., 386-371)


  Date: During the experience of Xenophon (399 to 355)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
A Spartan youth (male, adolescent, free, Spartan)
  Swearee:
The other members of the sworn band (male, any adult age, free, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: He could be part of a sworn band
  If refused: He could not be part of a sworn band
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That he would fight while he was alive, and would not regard being alive as more important than being free; that he would not leave his taxiarch or his enomotarch, whether he was alive or dead, and that he would bury the dead among his fellow-fighters, and that he would leave no-one unburied]
 
  Linguistic: wmot
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Xenophon describes the Spartan military organisation as comprising six morai of cavalry and infantry, which each of the morai commanded by one polemarch, four lochagoi, eight pentekonteres and sixteen enomotarchs i.e. "commanders of the sworn bands".
Xenophon goes on to say (in a slightly corrupt passage) that the morai form with the enomotiai sometimes two, three or six abreast.
See also oath id 1272.
