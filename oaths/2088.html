Oath ID 2088: Xenophon, Anabasis, 2.3.24, (literary, Hist., ca.370-355)


  Date: 401
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Persia   Location: Babylonia
 
  Swearer:
Heralds of King Artaxerxes II (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
Clearchus (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Heralds of King Artaxerxes II (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
Clearchus (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
Heralds of King Artaxerxes II
 
  If taken: The truce would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a truce
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would not attack each other]
 
  Linguistic: spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: After the truce was concluded, Tissaphernes came to address the Greeks to ask why they had taken the field against the King.
Clearchus responded that they had not gathered to fight against the King, and that they had taken the field because they were obliged to defend Cyrus because he had done them favours in the past.
Clearchus then says that since Cyrus is dead, they are no longer fighting against the King for his realm and have no wish to harm his territory or the King himself, but will defend themselves if attacked.
Clearchus adds that if anyone does them a service they will not be outdone in doing a service to him.
Tissaphernes responds that he will carry this message to the King and bring back his reply, stating that the truce will continue until he returns. He also proposes to set up a market so that the Greeks can gather provisions.
