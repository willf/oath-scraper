  Remarks: Demosthenes includes the fact that Meidias "stood beside the judges while they were taking their oath" among the many crimes committed by Meidias.
What Demosthenes says here will not make much sense to the jury unless they are already familiar with the story at which he is hinting and which he will refer to a little less obscurely later (3402). Even there, the story is not spelt out; but the author of the second Hypothesis writes: "As Demosthenes says..while the judges were swearing that they would give victory to the best singer [sic], Meidias kept nudging them and saying 'except Demosthenes', until Demosthenes shouted out and exposed him." It is not clear whether the commentator has inferred all this from the text of the speech, or whether he has some other source (e.g. a lost speech of another orator).
For further details see also oath id 3402.
