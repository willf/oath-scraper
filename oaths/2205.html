Oath ID 2205: Xenophon, Anabasis, 6.6.17, (literary, Hist., ca.370-355)

  Statement: [That neither Xenophon nor anyone else directed him to rescue the man who was arrested for stealing sheep]
Ἐγώ, ὦ ἄνδρες, ὄμνυμι θεοὺς καὶ θεὰς ἦ μὴν μήτε με Ξενοφῶντα κελεῦσαι ἀφελέσθαι τὸν ἄνδρα μήτε ἄλλον ὑμῶν μηδένα·
 
  Linguistic: omnu
 
  God(s) invoked:
The gods and goddesses    
 
  Remarks: This oath was taken at the time when Cleander, the Spartan harmost of Byzantium, had arrived in Bithynia with ships to transport the Greeks to the Hellespont.
Because Cleander arrived with only two triremes the Greeks feared that they would lose the many sheep that they had acquired in the mountain. As a result, they made an agreement with Dexippus one of the perioeci who was serving with Cleander that was intended to safeguard the livestock.
Dexippus then broke his agreement with the Greeks, and arrested one of them for stealing sheep.
When Dexippus was taking the man to Cleander, Agasias rescued him because he was a member of his lochos. The other soldiers of the lochos then set about stoning Dexippus, calling him a traitor.
When the fighting was stopped, Dexippus accused Xenophon of ordering Agasias to attack him.
Agasias then swore that no one had ordered him to do so, and offered to go to Cleander for trial.
