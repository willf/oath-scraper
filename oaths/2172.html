Oath ID 2172: Xenophon, Anabasis, 3.1.19-20, (literary, Hist., ca.370-355)


  Date: 401
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Persia   Location: Assyria
 
  Swearer:
Greek generals (male, mature, power-holder, various)
Greek lochagoi (male, mature, power-holder, various)
  Swearee:
Tissaphernes (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
The brother of the King's wife (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
 
  Proposed by:
Tissaphernes
 
  If taken: The treaty would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a treaty
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: see remarks
 
  Sanctifying features Clasping of right hands
 
  Statement: [That they would proceed as they would through a friendly country, doing no damage and taking no food and drink from the land unless the Persians did not provide them with a market, and if they did provide a market to obtain provisions by purchase]
 
  Linguistic: ork, spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: When Xenophon addresses the lochagoi of Proxenus the Boeotian he observes that "as long as the treaty lasted I never ceased commiserating ourselves and congratulating the King and his followers. For I saw plainly what a great amount of fine land they possessed, what an abundance of provisions, what quantities of servants, cattle, gold, and apparel.
But whenever I took thought of the situation of our own soldiers, I saw that we had no share in these good things, except we bought them, I knew there were but few of us who still had money wherewith to buy, and I knew that our oaths restrained us from getting provisions in any other way than by purchase. Hence, with these considerations in mind, I used sometimes to fear the truce more than I now fear war."
Xenophon goes on to say that he is no longer afraid because the Persians have violated their oaths (see oath id 2098).
