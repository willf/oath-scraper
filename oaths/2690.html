  Remarks: This is a discussion of the rights of a suppliant and the sanctity of oaths. The Athenian explains that if a suppliant is cheated out of an agreement, after having invoked a god as a witness, that god becomes his guardian and avenger. The man will never be wronged without vengeance being taken for his wrongs. The suppliant is assumed to have invoked a god as a witness to an agreement (homologia). That is, the person supplicated has sworn to protect the suppliant, and the suppliant has accepted this guarantee and has risen from his suppliant posture, thereby passing from the protection of Zeus Hikesios into that of the god(s) named in the oath. A neat example of such a sequence is that between Medea and Aegeus in Eur. Med. 709ff: Medea makes herself a suppliant (709-713); Aegeus promises to protect her, and backs up this promise with an oath dictated by Medea (719-755- see oath id 120); Medea releases him from her suppliantâ€™s hold (756).
