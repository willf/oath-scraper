Oath ID 198: Aeschylus, Septem Contra Thebas, 42-48, (literary, Trag., 467)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Thebes   Location: Outside the gates
 
  Swearer:
Polynices (male, mature, free, Theban)
Amphiaraus (male, mature, power-holder, Argive)
Parthenopaeus (male, adolescent, free, Arcadian)
Hippomedon (male, mature, power-holder, Argive)
Capaneus (male, mature, power-holder, Argive)
Eteoclus (male, mature, power-holder, Argive)
Tydeus (male, mature, free, Aetolian)
  Swearee:
World at large (n/a, n/a, unknown, n/a)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: Their dedication to the campaign is proven
  If refused: Their dedication to the campaign is not proven
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Battle is assured.
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features The throat of a bull is slit, its blood is caught in a black shield, and the swearers dip their fingers in the blood.
 
  Statement: [That they will either raze the city of the Cadmeans to the ground, sacking it by force, or that they will die and mix their blood with the earth]
ἄνδρες γὰρ ἑπτά, θούριοι λοχαγέται,
ταυροσφαγοῦντες ἐς μελάνδετον σάκος,
καὶ θιγγάνοντες χερσὶ ταυρείου φόνου,
῎Αρην ᾿Ενυὼ καὶ φιλαίματον Φόβον
ὡρκωμότησαν, ἢ πόλει κατασκαφὰς
θέντες λαπάξειν ἄστυ Καδμείων βίαι,
ἢ γῆν θανόντες τήνδε φυράσειν φόνωι·
 
  Linguistic: wrk
 
  God(s) invoked:
Ares    
Enyo    
Phobos Epithet(s) blood-loving
 
  Remarks: The Seven swear either of two things, to annihilate the city or to die (cf. oath id 293). So death here will not be a consequence of breaking the oath, it is part of the oath itself. Compare the comic treatment of a "slaughter" over a shield in Aristophanes' Lysistrata (see oath id 1164), and note also the description of oath rituals among the Scythians by Herodotus (see oath id 1327). Oath sacrifices are performed over a shield also at oath id 2078. The oath statement of the Seven is similar to that of the Spartans attacking Messene, see oath id 3599.
