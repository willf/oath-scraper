Oath ID 1649: Thucydides, History, 4.15.2-16.3, (literary, Hist., 403-395)


  Date: 425
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Pylos   Location: The Athenian fortifications
 
  Swearer:
Generals (male, mature, power-holder, Athenian)
Spartan magistrates (ta telh) (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Generals (male, mature, power-holder, Athenian)
Spartan magistrates (ta telh) (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
The Spartan magistrates
 
  If taken: The truce would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a truce
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: Sixty ships were delivered up and the Spartan envoys went to Athens
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following terms:
1. The Spartans were to surrender to the Athenians the ships in which they had fought the battle;
2. The Spartans were to bring to Pylos all the other ships of war in Laconia;
3. Tthe Spartans were not to attack the fortification either by land or sea;
4. The Athenians were to permit the Spartans on the mainland to send a set amount of food to the men on the island under Athenian supervision (no boat was to sail there in secret);
5. The Athenians were to go on guarding the island as before, but without landing on it and were not to attack the army of the Peloponnesians either by land or sea;
6. If either party should violate this agreement in any particular whatsoever the truce would be at an end;
7. The truce was to hold good until the Spartan envoys should return from Athens (the Athenians would conduct these in a trireme and bring them back);
8. On the return of the envoys the Athenians were to restore the ships to the Spartans in as good condition as when they received them]
 
  Linguistic: speis, spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Thucydides states that the Spartans sent magistrates to Pylos to examine the situation. These magistrates decided that the situation was so perilous that they ought to conclude a truce.
Accordingly they proposed a truce with the Athenian generals so that they could send envoys to Athens to propose an agreement. The Athenian generals accepted their proposal and concluded a truce on the terms described in the statement.
See also oath id 1650.
