  Remarks: Thucydides states that after the envoys from Syracuse and Athens put to the Camarinaeans their arguments as to why they should support them in the war, the Camarianeans resolved to help neither the Athenians nor the Syracusans, as they were allies of both parties, and it seemed consistent with their oaths to aid neither party.
Thucydides does not state when this alliance took place. The relationship between Syracuse and Camarina during this period can be summarised as follows:
1. In 427 Camarina was hostile to Syracuse and made an alliance with Athens
2. In 425 (see 4.25) Camarina was betrayed to Syracuse by a certain Archias
3. In 424 the Sicilians agreed to make peace with each other (see oath id 1654)
4. In 422 Camarina was again hostile to Syracuse
5. In 415 (see 6.52) the Camarinaeans warned the Athenians that Syracuse was manning a fleet and told the Athenians that they would help them if they came (and then rebuffed them)
6. In 415 (see 6.67.2) cavalrymen and archers from Camarina served alongside the Syracusans against the Athenians
7. In 415 (6.88.2) the Camarinaeans claimed to be allies of both the Syracusans and Athenians and to have exchanged oaths with both.
