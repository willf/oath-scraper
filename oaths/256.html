  Remarks: This inscription records an alliance between Athens and Perdiccas, King of Macedon and his allies, and Arrhabaeus the Lyncestian and his allies.
The terms of the oath are very fragmentary, but it is clear that Perdiccas and the Athenians are to have the same friends and enemies, Athens will have exclusive rights to take timber for oars from Macedon, and the agreement includes peaceful relations between Perdiccas and Arrhabaeus the Lyncestian in its terms.
It appears that the Athenians compelled the entire Macedonian nobility to swear the oath alongside Perdiccas to ensure his loyalty. Numerous others swore the oath, but their names are too fragmentary to be included in the database. This entry takes into account textual changes from SEG 25.18.
For other references to alliances between Perdiccas and Athens see oath id 257, 1544, 1546, 1568, 1679, 1682, 1755.
