Oath ID 3322: Demosthenes, On the Treaty with Alexander, 12, (literary, Orat., 335)


  Date: 338/7 (338)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Corinth   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
Representatives of Greek states except Sparta (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
  Swearee:
Philip of Macedon (male, mature, power-holder, Macedonian)
 
  Proposed by:
Philip of Macedon?
 
  If taken: They would be party to the peace and alliance
  If refused: They would not be party to the peace and alliance
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would abide by the peace(?)and neither break the agreement with Philip (?)nor take up arms for harm against any of those who abide by the oaths (?)neither by land nor by sea; nor would they take any polis or guardpost nor harbour, for war, of any of those participating in the peace, by any craft or contrivance; nor would they overthrow the kingdom of Philip or his descendants, nor the constitutions existing in each state when they swore the oaths concerning the peace; nor would they do anything contrary to these agreements, nor allow anyone else as far as possible; if anyone did commit any breach of treaty concerning the agreements, they would go in support as called on by those who are wronged (?)and would make war against the one who transgresses the common peace (?)as decided by the common synedrion and the hegemon; and they would not abandon..]
 
  Linguistic: ork

  Remarks: The speaker complains that those in the pay of the Macedonian king are so insolent that they insist the Athenians should abide by oaths which have already been broken, as if Alexander's power allows him to commit perjury.
