Oath ID 642: Aristophanes, Birds, 440-447, (literary, Comic., 414)


  Date: 414
  Reference type: Oath
  State: unknown   Location: Far from Athens
 
  Swearer:
Chorus-leader (n/a, mature, free, n/a)
  Swearee:
Peisetaerus (male, old, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Peisetaerus
 
  If taken: Peisetaerus will reveal his plan
  If refused: Peisetaerus will not reveal his plan
  If kept: The chorus will be judged victorious by all the judges and the audience
  If broken: The chorus will win by just one vote
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Peisetaerus disarms and reveals his plan
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: [That the Chorus will not bite Peisetaerus nor pull at his balls, nor poke him in the eyes ]
ἥνπερ ὁ πίθηκος τῇ γυναικὶ διέθετο,
ὁ μαχαιροποιός, μήτε δάκνειν τούτους ἐμὲ
μήτ’ ὀρχίπεδ’ ἕλκειν μήτ’ ὀρύττειν—
{ΕΥ.} Οὔ τί που
τόνδ’;
{ΠΙ.} Οὐδαμῶς. Οὔκ, ἀλλὰ τὠφθαλμὼ λέγω.
{ΧΟ.} Διατίθεμαι ’γώ.
{ΠΙ.} Κατόμοσόν νυν ταῦτά μοι.
{ΧΟ.} Ὄμνυμ’ ἐπὶ τούτοις, πᾶσι νικᾶν τοῖς κριταῖς
καὶ τοῖς θεαταῖς πᾶσιν, —
{ΠΙ.} Ἔσται ταυταγί.
{ΧΟ.} εἰ δὲ παραβαίην, ἑνὶ κριτῇ νικᾶν μόνον.
 
  Linguistic: omnu, omos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: In the terms and conditions of this oath, the Chorus-leader is clearly referring to the competitive nature of comic performances in Athens. Note that the rewards and penalties specified are designed to make it impossible for the play not to be victorious - the worst penalty for breaking the oath will be to win by just one vote. The plan which Peisetaerus will reveal is for the creation of a bird city in the sky, endorsed by Tereus at 194-195; cf. oath id 635.
