  Remarks: The date is confirmed by the presence of Dor[otheus] as demarch in line 4. Dorotheus is known to have been demarch in 326/5 from IG II(2) 1198.
Fragment a indicates that those lessees who claimed to have reason to not pay their rent (and had thus become debtors) could submit to arbitration by the demesmen. They would swear an oath before entering the dikasterion to submit their case for arbitration and defer payment until the demesmen might be in position to make a judgement. They were also to swear when they submitted their case to abide by whatever decision the demesmen might make and to pay back the money if required, or provide a guarantor.
The oath itself is referred to in fragment a lines 8-13 and survives in fragmentary form in fragment b.
