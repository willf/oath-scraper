Oath ID 3838: IG I(3), 83, 26-28, (inscriptional, Public, ca.420)


  Date: ca.420 (421 to 420)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Elis   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Demiourgoi (male, mature/old, power-holder, Elean)
  Swearee:
Thesmophylakes (male, mature/old, power-holder, Elean)
 
  Proposed by:
Thesmophylakes
 
  If taken: Their alliance would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be an alliance
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features Full-grown sacrificial victims
 
  Statement: [That they will stand by this alliance according to its terms, justly, innocently, and guilelessly, and will not transgress it in any way or by any means whatsoever]
ἐμμενῶ τῇ ξυμμαχίᾳ κατὰ τὰ ξυγκείμενα δικαίως καὶ ἀβλαβῶς καὶ ἀδόλως, καὶ οὐ παραβήσομαι τέχνῃ οὐδὲ μηχανῇ οὐδεμιᾷ.
 
  Linguistic: omnu, omos, summac
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: This inscription records the so-called Argive alliance. See Thucydides 5.47.1-12 for full details.
The text indicates that each city will swear the oath that is most binding in their land. The Athenians were to swear on behalf of their allies, whereas the Argives, Mantineans, Eleans, and their allies were to swear individually by state.
It is stipulated that for the renewal of the oath the Athenians will travel to Elis, Mantinea, and Argos, thirty days before the Olympic games, and the Argives, Eleans and Mantineans will travel to Athens ten days before the Great Panathenaea.
For this system of alliances, which Thucydides describes as the "Argive alliance," see also oath id 251.
