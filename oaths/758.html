  Remarks: This text is an example of pederastic graffiti on stones that were later used in the gymnasium at Thera.
The first line reads: "Pheidippides fucked. Timagoras and Empheres and me - we got fucked too" (Πhειδιπίδας ὦιπhε. Τιμαγόρας καὶ Ἐνπhέρες καὶ ἐγὄιπh[ομες]).
A certain Empylos then wrote "Empylos [did?] this [too]" (Ἔνπυλος τάδε).
This line was not completed, and someone else wrote "faggot" (πόρνος).
Lastly, perhaps the in line one, Empedocles writes, "Empedocles wrote this. And he danced, by Apollo!" (Ἐνπεδοκλῆς ἐνεϞόπτετο τάδε Ϟὠρκ<h>ε<ῖ>το μὰ τὸν Ἀπόλ<λ>).
It seems that the verb ὀρχέομαι means more than "dance" in this context. The verb certainly can have a sexual meaning; cf. Euripides, Cyclops 171 (which, if understood literally, would be sadly anticlimactic). B.B. Powell," Why was the Greek alphabet invented?", ClAnt 8, (1989), p345, writes, "This youthful pederastic boaster not only writes - he dances too!".
