Oath ID 3482: Ctesias, Persika, Fr. 27.68 Jacoby, (literary, Hist., 398-383)


  Date: 401
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Persia   Location: Assyria
 
  Swearer:
Tissaphernes (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
  Swearee:
Clearchus (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: Clearchus and his men would be persuaded to come to the conference
  If refused: Clearchus and his men might not be persuaded to come to the conference
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: no
  Impact: Clearchus and his men are presuaded to come
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That, if Clearchus came with his generals to Tissaphernes, he would give Clearchus publicly the names of those who told him that Clearchus was plotting against him and his army]
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The reference to the oaths and deceit in this fragment of Ctesias strongly suggests that the oaths were somehow connected to luring Clearchus and his generals to Tissaphernes' conference where they were then killed. The oath statement quoted here is based on an agreement referred to in Xenophon Anabasis 2.5.24-26. In an attempt to quell Clearchus' suspicions, Tissaphernes proposes this agreement. Clearchus then agrees to come to the conference and to reveal the sources from whom he has his reports concerning Tissaphernes. It must be noted, however, that the agreement is not sworn in Xenophon, although Tissaphernes does in any case break a previous oath not to injure Clearchus (see oath id 2111). The deceitful Menon is also mentioned in the Ctesias passage as being involved. Cf. oath id 2166.
