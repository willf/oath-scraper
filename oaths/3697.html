  Remarks: The speaker states that he offered Callicles' mother the chance to swear to her son's versions of the fact, and declared that he was ready to summon his own mother to swear to his version (see also oath id 3682).
Presumably the challenge (actually delivered to Callicles himself, not directly to his mother) will have specified that if the oath was tendered to her and she refused it, Callicles would lose his case; more precisely, since the speaker asks for only one challenge (not two) to be read to the jury, Callicles would lose his case if his mother refused to swear while the speakerâ€™s own mother did not refuse. Callicles either rejected the challenge outright or (perhaps more likely) haggled over the terms so that no agreement was reached.
