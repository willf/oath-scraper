Oath ID 3649: Theopompus, Philippica, Fr. 153 Jacoby, (literary, Hist., 324-322)


  Date: 479
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Plataea   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Greek hoplites (male, adolescent/mature, free, various)
  Swearee:
Greek hoplites (male, adolescent/mature, free, various)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: unknown
  If refused: unknown
  If kept: May their polis be free from sickness, and remain unsacked; may the land bear fruit; may women bear children like to their parents; may cattle bear young like to cattle
  If broken: May their polis be afflicted with sickness and be sacked; may the land be barren; may the women and the cattle bear monsters
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features Blood sacrifices covered with shields (?)
 
  Statement: [That they would fight while they lived, and not put life before being free, and not desert the taxiarch nor the enomotarch, neither while they live nor when they are dead, and that they would not depart unless the leaders led the way, and they would do whatever the generals command, and they would bury in the same place the dead of those who were allied, and they would leave no one unburied, and when they had been victorious fighting against the barbarians they would (totally destroy and) dedicate a tenth of the city of the Thebans, and that they would not raze Athens or Sparta or Plataea or any of the other poleis that were allied, and not keep them from running water, whether they were friends or enemies]
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Here Theopompus claims that the so-called oath of Plataea is, in fact, spurious. This text was set up in the mid-fourth century. See oath id 338 for further details.
