  Remarks: Xenophon states that the Corinthians sent envoys to Thebes to see if a peace could be negotiated. When the Thebans agreed, the Corinthians requested that they might conclude peace with those who desired peace and leave the war to those who wished to continue fighting.
When the Thebans gave their consent to this as well, the Corinthians asked permission from the Spartans to make peace with Thebes. The Spartans advised them to make peace and gave permission to their other allies to make peace with Thebes as well. They refused to make peace themselves while Messenia was independent.
The Thebans tried to force the Corinthians to make an alliance with them (see oath id 2038), but the Corinthians refused.
Xenophon observes that after the peace was concluded the Argives, having sworn to make peace on terms that each party should keep its own territory, and fearing that the Phliasian exiles occupying Tricaranum - in Phliasian territory - would therefore have to evacuate it, took over the place and maintained a garrison there, claiming it was their own territory (even though not long before they had been treating it as enemy soil and ravaging the land), and refused a Phliasian demand for arbitration.
