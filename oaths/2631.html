  Remarks: Aeschines is following the flawed historical narrative from Andocides, On the Peace with the Spartans, 3-5. (See oath id 2383). It is typical of Andocides' historical inaccuracy.
Aeschines and Andocides both state Miltiades, the son of Cimon negotiated a fifty year treaty with the Spartans, by which the Athenians abided for thirteen years.
This, however, was not the case. It was Cimon the son of Miltiades who was ostracised (461) and negotiated a peace with the Spartans (451/0).
This truce actually lasted for five years.
Both Aeschines and Andocides state that the Athenians gained the following benefits from the peace:
1. they fortified the Piraeus;
2. they built the northern Long Wall;
3. they built one hundred triremes to replace the old ones;
4. they established a force of three hundred horsemen;
5. they purchased the three hundred Scythian archers.
See also oath id 1542.
