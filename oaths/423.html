Oath ID 423: Homer, Iliad, 23.581-585, (literary, Epic., date uncertain)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Troy   Location: Greek camp
 
  Swearer:
Antilochus (male, adolescent, power-holder, Pylian)
  Swearee:
Achaeans (male, mature, free, Achaean)
 
  Proposed by:
Menelaus
 
  If taken: Antilochus will get the prize
  If refused: Antilochus will not get the prize
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: no   True: n/a
  Impact: n/a
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features Hand to be placed on horses and horse-whip (representative of Poseidon)
 
  Statement: [That he did not cheat in the chariot race]
— Ἀντίλοχ’ εἰ δ’ ἄγε δεῦρο διοτρεφές, ἣ θέμις ἐστί,
στὰς ἵππων προπάροιθε καὶ ἅρματος, αὐτὰρ ἱμάσθλην
χερσὶν ἔχε ῥαδινήν, ᾗ περ τὸ πρόσθεν ἔλαυνες,
ἵππων ἁψάμενος γαιήοχον ἐννοσίγαιον
ὄμνυθι μὴ μὲν ἑκὼν τὸ ἐμὸν δόλῳ ἅρμα πεδῆσαι.
 
  Linguistic: omnu
 
  God(s) invoked:
Poseidon Epithet(s) earth-moving
 
  Remarks: This is the same oath referred to at id 421. It is refused by Antilochus who concedes and offers full restitution to Menelaus, suggesting that he had, in fact, cheated (though not giving up his claim to be rightfully entitled to the prize). Menelaus accepts his offer of restitution.
