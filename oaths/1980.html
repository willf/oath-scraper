Oath ID 1980: Sophocles, Electra, 1063-1065, (literary, Trag., date uncertain)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Argos   Location: Palace of Agamemnon
 
  Swearer:
Chorus (female) (n/a, mature, free, Argive)
  Swearee:
World at large (n/a, n/a, unknown, n/a)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: Emphasises the statement
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: [That they, if they do not pay their dues, will not go long without suffering]
ἀλλ’ οὐ τὰν Διὸς ἀστραπὰν
καὶ τὰν οὐρανίαν Θέμιν
δαρὸν οὐκ ἀπόνητοι.
 
  Linguistic: ou + acc.
 
  God(s) invoked:
Lightning-bolt of Zeus    
Themis    
 
  Remarks: March rightly points out that the implicit subject of this very elliptical sentence is not so much “those who do not pay their dues” as “we, if we do not pay our dues” (cf. telou=men). The informal oath formula οὐ + acc., current in Doric and Arcadian (cf. oath ids 575, 576, 1217, 1219, 1230, 2214, 2307), was not used in Attic, and in tragedy it is found only in Sophocles (see oath ids 244, 1978, 1979) and mostly, as here, in lyric.
