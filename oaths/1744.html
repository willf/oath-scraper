Oath ID 1744: Thucydides, History, 5.66.3, (literary, Hist., 403-395)


  Date: 431-400 i.e. the period that Thucydides describes (431 to 400)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
A Spartan youth (male, adolescent, free, Spartan)
  Swearee:
The other members of the sworn band (male, any adult age, free, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: He could be part of a sworn band
  If refused: He could not be part of a sworn band
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That he would fight while he was alive, and would not regard being alive as more important than being free; that he would not leave his taxiarch or his enomotarch, whether he was alive or dead, and that he would bury the dead among his fellow-fighters, and that he would leave no-one unburied]
 
  Linguistic: wmot
 
  God(s) invoked:
 
  Remarks: Thucydides describes the Spartan chain of command, whereby the king gives orders to the polemarchs, the polemarchs to the lochagoi, the lochagoi to the pentekosteres, the pentekosteres to the enomotarchs, and the enomotarchs to the enomotiai i.e. the "sworn bands" that each Spartan joined as an adolescent.
Thucydides states that at the battle at Mantinea each Spartan enomotia was deployed to a depth of four men. What Thucydides seems to be saying is that while the depth of the phalanx was at the discretion of the lochos commander, the normal depth was eight; if so, an enomotia would normally have 32 men, a pentekostus (despite its name) 128, and a lochos 512.
See also oath id 1272.
