Oath ID 1653: Thucydides, History, 4.46.3-47.1, (literary, Hist., 403-395)


  Date: 425/4 (425)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Corcyra   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Generals (male, mature, power-holder, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Corcyraean oligarchs (male, mature/old, free, Corcyraean)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: The truce would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a truce
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: see remarks
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would conduct the oligarchs to Athens for trial and safeguard them until the Athenian demos had decided their fate]
 
  Linguistic: spond
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Thucydides states that the Athenians and Corcyraeans mounted an expedition against the oligarchs who had established themselves on Mt Istone.
The combined forces captured the stronghold, but the oligarchs fled to higher ground.
These soon capitulated on condition that they should surrender their mercenary troops, give up their arms, and leave it to the Athenian demos to decide their fate.
Thucydides states that the Athenian generals conveyed the men under truce (ὑποσπόνδους) to the island of Ptychia to be kept under custody until they should be sent to Athens. However, the understanding was that if any oligarch should be caught trying to escape the truce should be regarded as broken by all.
The leaders of the Corcyraean demos feared that the oligarchs would not be put to death, and sent men to tell them that they ought to escape because the Athenian generals were intending to hand them over to the Corcyraean demos. When they were persuaded and caught sailing away the truce was broken and the whole party was handed over to the Corcyraean demos. They were brutally slain. The details of their deaths can be found in 4.47.2-48.5.
