Oath ID 2992: Isocrates, Panathenaicus, 103, (literary, Orat., 342)


  Date: 412/11 (412)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Persia   Location: The plain of Maeander
 
  Swearer:
Tissaphernes (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
Representatives (male, any adult age, power-holder, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Tissaphernes (male, mature, power-holder, Persian)
Representatives (male, any adult age, power-holder, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: The treaty would be formalised
  If refused: There would not be a treaty
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: more complex
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following termss:
1. The King's country in Asia is to remain his possession and to be governed as he pleases
2. The Spartans and their allies shall not go against the country of the King, nor the King against the Spartans and their allies.
3. If any of the Spartans and their allies go against the King's territory, the Spartans and their allies shall stop them, and if any of the King's subjects go against the Spartans and their allies the King shall stop them
4. Tissaphernes shall pay for the ships now present until the arrival of the King's ships. After these arrive, the Spartans can then pay for their own ships if they wish. If not, Tissaphernes will provide money, but the Spartans will repay the money at the end of the war
5. When the King's ships arrive, the ships of the Spartans and their allies and those of the King shall wage war together, according to what seems best to Tissaphernes and the Spartans and their allies. If they wish to end the war with the Athenians, they will do so jointly]
 
  Linguistic: summac
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Isocrates describes how the Spartans were so avid to gain mastery of the sea that they were (a) negotiating a treaty of friendship and alliance with the Persian king, and promising to give over to him all the Greeks of Asia, and (b) promising to liberate Athens' [Asian] allies if they revolted.
He claims that after they had given these pledges to the allies of Athens and the King, and had conquered Athens in war, they reduced those who they had sworn to set free to a state of slavery worse than that of Helots, and they returned the favour to the King by persuading his brother Cyrus to dispute his throne and sent an army to support Cyrus in his endeavour. (This is massively misleading: Cyrus needed no Spartan persuasion to dispute his brother's throne; his brother wsa not the King [Darius II] with whom Sparta had made the alliance, but his successor [Artaxerxes II]; and the Greek army that supported him was neither despatched nor financed by Sparta.)
See also oath id 1818.
