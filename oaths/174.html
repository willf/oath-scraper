Oath ID 174: Euripides, Phoenissae, 1006, (literary, Trag., ?409/408)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Thebes   Location: Outside the palace
 
  Swearer:
Menoceus (male, adolescent, free, Theban)
  Swearee:
Chorus (female) (n/a, adolescent, free, Phoenician)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Strengthens Menoceus' resolve to sacrifice himself for the city
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: [That he shall not betray his father, brother and city and leave the land like a coward]
ἐγὼ δέ, πατέρα καὶ κασίγνητον προδοὺς
πόλιν τ’ ἐμαυτοῦ, δειλὸς ὣς ἔξω χθονὸς
ἄπειμ’, ὅπου δ’ ἂν ζῶ κακὸς φανήσομαι;
μὰ τὸν μετ’ ἄστρων Ζῆν’ ῎Αρη τε φοίνιον,
ὃς τοὺς ὑπερτείλαντας ἐκ γαίας ποτὲ
Σπαρτοὺς ἄνακτας τῆσδε γῆς ἱδρύσατο.
 
  Linguistic: ma ton
 
  God(s) invoked:
Zeus Epithet(s) among the stars
Ares Epithet(s) murderous
 
  Remarks: This oath marks Menoeceus' decision to sacrifice himself for Thebes, the sacrifice of a male in this way being a unique moment in tragedy.
