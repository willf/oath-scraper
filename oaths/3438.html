Oath ID 3438: Hellanicus, The First Trojan War, Fr. 26b Jacoby, (literary, Hist., 460-395)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Troy   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Laomedon (male, adolescent/mature, power-holder, Trojan)
  Swearee:
Apollo (male, mature, power-holder, n/a)
Poseidon (male, mature, power-holder, n/a)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: Apollo and Poseidon would build the walls of Troy
  If refused: Apollo and Poseidon would not build the walls of Troy
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: no
  Impact: Walls of Troy are built
  Consequences of breach: Poseidon sends a sea monster which destroys both the people it met with and the crops
 
  Sanctifying features Oath sworn to gods themselves
 
  Statement: [That he would pay Apollo and Poseidon an agreed wage for building the walls of Troy]
Λαομέδων δὲ παραβὰς τοὺς ὅρκους καὶ τὰς συνθήκας μὴ δοὺς τὸν μισθὸν ἀπήλασεν αὐτούς.
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
Apollo    
Poseidon    
 
  Remarks: Laomedon was known for his treachery, but here the breaking of his agreement with Apollo and Poseidon is treated as the violation of an oath. This is also the case in Virgil Geogics 1.501-2 (satis iam pridem sanguine nostro/ Laomedonteae luimus periuria Troiae). Cf. also Aeneid 4.541-2 (Dido speaking) nescis heu, perdita, necdum / Laomedonteae sentis periuria gentis? In Ovid Met. 11.205-6 it is implied, rather than stated, that Laomedon's perjury consisted in swearing that he had never promised to pay the two gods; Hellanicus, however, clearly assumes that the initial promise was itself made on oath.
