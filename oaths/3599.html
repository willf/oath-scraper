Oath ID 3599: Ephorus, Histories, Fr. 216 Jacoby, (literary, Hist., 360-330)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Spartans (male, any adult age, free, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Spartans (male, any adult age, free, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: They will prove their dedication to fighting Messene
  If refused: They will not prove their dedication to fighting Messene
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: Dedication to the war with Messene is assured
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they would not return home again until they had either destroyed Messene or were all killed]
ὀμόσαντες μὴ πρότερον ἐπανήξειν οἴκαδε πρὶν ἢ Μεσσήνην ἀνελεῖν ἢ πάντας ἀποθανεῖν
 
  Linguistic: omos, ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The oath sworn here causes a problem among the women in Sparta who, after ten years, complain about the length of time that their husbands have been away. In order to keep their oath but also keep their women having children, they send back the youngest men among them, since these would have been too young at the time of the initial expedition to have taken part in the oaths themselves. The oath statement itself is remarkably similar to that sworn by the Seven before they attack Thebes, see oath id 198. Cf. oath id 2883 where the Argives swear that no Argive man will grow his hair long, and none of their women will wear gold jewellery, until they recover Thyrea.
