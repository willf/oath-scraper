Oath ID 278: IG I(3), 1453, 10, (inscriptional, Public, 450-446 or 425/4 or ante 414)


  Date: 450-446 or 425/4 or ante 414 (450 to 414)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: Bouleuterion
 
  Swearer:
Bouleutai (male, mature, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Presiding magistrate (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
Presiding magistrate
 
  If taken: They can take office in the boule
  If refused: They cannot take office in the boule
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: unknown
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they will give counsel in accordance with the laws;
That they will give the best counsel to the polis/to the demos of the Athenians;
That they will not imprison any Athenian citizen who offers three sureties taxed in the same class as themselves, <except any person found guilty of conspiring to betray the city or to overthrow the democracy (added post 411?)>, or any tax-farmer or his surety or collector being in default;
That they will neither exile, nor imprison, nor put to death anybody without trial;
That if someone coins money of silver in the poleis and does not use Athenian coins or weights or measures, but uses foreign coins and weights and measures, they shall impeach him according to the former decree proposed by Clearchus (added ca.448);
That they are not less than thirty years old;
That they will not put to the vote anything contrary to the laws]
 
  Linguistic: ork
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: This oath is an addition to the bouleutic oath. Each new bouleutes now also swore: "If someone coins money of silver in the poleis and does not use Athenian coins or weights or measures, but uses foreign coins and weights and measures, I shall impeach him according to the former decree proposed by Clearchus" (ἐὰν τις κόπτηι νόμισ[μα] ἀργυρίο ἐν τῆσι πό[λεσι] καὶ μὴ χρῆται νομ[ίσμασιν τοῖς] Ἀθη[να]ίων ἢ σταθμοῖς ἢ μέτ[ροις ἀλλὰ ξενικοῖς νομίσμασι]ν καὶ σταθμοῖς καὶ [μ]έτροις, [ἐσαγγελλέσθω ἐς] τ[ὴν βολὴν κατὰ τὸ πρότε]ρον ψήφισμα ὅ Κλέαρχ[ος εἶπεν])
This addition to the bouleutic oath seems to be an enhancement of an earlier clause added to the oath (see oath id 3759).
This entry takes into account textual changes in SEG 28.2.
See also oath id 1775.
