  Remarks: It is decided that in Magnesia, since men's belief in the gods is not what it once was (cf. oath id 2727), litigants and defendants will be forbidden from swearing antomosiai and the like at the beginning of a trial. Rather each litigant and each defendant will write down his charges or defence and hand them to the magistrate without an oath. In all cases where a large gain will accrue to him who denies knowledge or swears an exomosia, all the contending parties must be judged by trial without oaths. Any oath which can be taken to gain an advantage for oneself is to be banned also during the trial (see oath id 2734). The prohibitions are instituted because of the high degree of perjury among those who do not hold the gods in respect (see oath id 2729). Only resident aliens are exempt from these prohibitions of oaths, since they are not expected to grow old in Magnesia.
