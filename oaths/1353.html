Oath ID 1353: Herodotus, The Histories, 6.69, (literary, Hist., 428-415)


  Date: During the reign of King Ariston ca. 545-530 (545 to 530)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Sparta   Location: Ariston's house
 
  Swearer:
Ariston's wife/Demaratus' mother (female, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
  Swearee:
Ariston (male, mature, power-holder, Spartan)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a

  Statement: [That Ariston himself had given her the garlands she was wearing]
Ἐγὼ δὲ ἐφάμην ἐκεῖνον· ὁ δὲ οὐκ ὑπεδέκετο· ἐγὼ δὲ κατωμνύμην
 
  Linguistic: omnu, wmnu
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Ariston's wife claimed that on the third night after Ariston brought her to his home a phantom appeared in his image and lay with her and placed garlands upon her.
Ariston entered later and asked who had given her the garlands. She said that he had given them to her, and when he denied this she swore an oath to confirm that he had given the garland to her.
The seers determined that the garlands had come from the precinct of the hero Astrabacus which was established at the courtyard doors.
Ariston's wife thus claimed that either the hero or Ariston was the father of Demaratus.
