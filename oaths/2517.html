Oath ID 2517: Deinarchus, Against Demosthenes, 28, (literary, Orat., 323)


  Date: 346
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens, Pella   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Philip of Macedon (male, mature, power-holder, Macedonian)
Philip of Macedon's representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Macedonian)
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
Representatives of the allies of Athens (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
  Swearee:
Philip of Macedon (male, mature, power-holder, Macedonian)
Philip of Macedon's representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Macedonian)
Representatives (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
Representatives of the allies of Athens (male, mature/old, power-holder, various)
 
  Proposed by:
Philocrates
 
  If taken: Peace treaty formalised
  If refused: Hostilities will continue
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: see remarks
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [? That they would abide by the following terms:
1. Both parties recognised the territory of the other;
2. There was to be an alliance and peace between Philip and the Athenians, with no time limit;
3. The peace and the alliance was to be binding to the allies of both parties;
4. The seas were to be kept safe i.e. neither party was to support pirates]
 
  Linguistic: eirhn
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: The prosecutor attacks Demosthenes for once helping to defend Philocrates who proposed the peace with Philip.
