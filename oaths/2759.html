Oath ID 2759: Hypereides, In defence of Lycophron, frag. IV b, col. 6, (literary, Orat., 333)


  Date: 350 to 333
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:

  Swearee:
Lycophron (male, mature, free, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown

  Statement: [That she would not have sex with anyone else]
ὅτι συνομωμο[κ̣υῖα εἰ]η πρὸς ἐμέ,
 
  Linguistic: omwm, ork, wmos
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: Lycophron has been charged with committing adultery with the daughter of Dioxippus, the wife of Charippus. Charippus has alleged that the daughter of Dioxippus sworn herself to Lycophron, and Lycophron was overheard urging her to abide by this oath.
Lycophron asks the jurors if they believe that Charippus was so obtuse as to be prepared to marry the girl if she had sworn an oath to him (Lycophron) and if he had been overheard urging her to abide by that oath.
