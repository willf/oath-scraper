Oath ID 1814: Aristotle, Athenaion Politeia, 67.1.4, (literary, Phil., 335-322)


  Date: Time of writing of Ath. Pol. (340 to 330)
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Athens   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Litigants (male, any adult age, free, Athenian)
  Swearee:
Presiding magistrate (male, mature/old, power-holder, Athenian)
 
  Proposed by:
unknown
 
  If taken: They will be allowed to proceed with their case
  If refused: They will not be allowed to proceed with their case
  If kept: unknown
  If broken: unknown
 
  Taken: yes   True: unknown
  Impact: Focus on the matter in hand
  Consequences of breach: unknown
 
  Sanctifying features unknown
 
  Statement: [That they will speak only about the matter in dispute]
κ[α]ὶ δ[ιο]μνύ[ουσι]ν οἱ ἀντίδικοι εἰς αὐτὸ τὸ πρᾶγμ[α] ἐρεῖν·
 
  Linguistic: omnu
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown    
 
  Remarks: This is part of the litigant's oath discussed in full by Gagarin in Sommerstein and Fletcher ed. Horkos (Exeter, 2007) 39-47. Of course, what is relevant to the matter in dispute is highly subjective. The relevancy oath, and the extent to which and ways in which it was (shall we say) stretched in practice in various types of trial, are discussed by Rhodes in Harris & Rubinstein ed. The Law and the Courts in Ancient Greece (2004) 137-158. See also id 2470 and argumentation in Antiphon 6.9.
