Oath ID 55: Carcinus, Medea, fr. novum, (literary, Trag., 380-340)


  Date: mythical time
  Reference type: Oath
  State: Corinth   Location: unknown
 
  Swearer:
Medea (female, mature, free, Colchian)
  Swearee:
Jason (male, mature, free, Iolcian)
Creon (male, mature, power-holder, Corinthian)
 
  Proposed by:
n/a
 
  If taken: n/a
  If refused: n/a
  If kept: n/a
  If broken: n/a
 
  Taken: yes   True: yes
  Impact: not believed
  Consequences of breach: n/a
 
  Sanctifying features none
 
  Statement: That Medea has not killed her children, but has sent them to safety, entrusting them to her nurse
 
  Linguistic: omnu
 
  God(s) invoked:
unknown Epithet(s) Scythian(?)
 
  Remarks: This oath appears in a Louvre papyrus (Antiq. Égypt. inv. E 10534) first published by A. Bélis, CRAI (2004) fasc. 3.1305-29 and re-edited by M.L. West, ZPE 161 (2007) 1-10. Bélis identified the passage as coming from Carcinus' Medea, in which, according to Aristotle (Rhetoric 1400b8ff), Medea, exactly as here, is accused of killing her children after they had disappeared, having "made a mistake in sending them away". In the fragment, Jason demands of Medea, who denies killing her children, that she produce them alive. Medea, who evidently cannot do so, instead makes the sworn statement above; the name of the god(dess) she invoked is lost, but the first few letters of an epithet are uncertainly read as Skuthik- (Scythian). The next to speak, probably Creon, ignores the oath entirely; Medea, he says, admits to having killed Glauce (Creon's daughter and Jason's new bride), she has surely killed her children too, and he gives Jason permission to put her to death. It is quite possible that the children have in fact, by this point, already perished at other hands, but if so this is not yet known to any of those on stage. The fragment is in iambic trimeters, but Medea's lines (and only hers) have musical notation; West argues that the papyrus is not part of a complete text of the play, but an excerpt arranged for "concert" performance by a virtuoso singer.
